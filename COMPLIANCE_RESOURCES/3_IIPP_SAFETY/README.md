# PACKAGE 03: CAL/OSHA IIPP + REQUIRED SAFETY TRAINING

## Overview

This package contains a complete, Cal/OSHA-compliant Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP) and three required safety training modules for California employers.

**Legal Authority:**
- California Labor Code § 6401.7 — IIPP requirement (every employer, 1+ employees)
- 8 CCR § 3203 — Cal/OSHA IIPP standard
- 8 CCR § 3395 — Heat Illness Prevention
- 8 CCR § 3220 — Emergency Action Plan
- 8 CCR § 14300 — Injury and Illness Recording
- SB 513 (eff. Jan. 1, 2026) — Training records = personnel records

**Enforcement Agency:** Cal/OSHA | 1-800-963-9424 | www.dir.ca.gov/dosh

## Implementation Steps

### Before You Begin (Customize the IIPP)

1. Fill in Program Administrator name and contact info
2. Fill in backup administrator
3. Complete Appendix A (worksite locations)
4. Complete Appendix B (emergency contacts — specific to your facility)
5. Review Section 4 (Hazard Assessment) — add your actual worksites
6. Review Section 7 (Training) — confirm applicable training types for your industry

### For Each Training Session

1. Read the facilitator guide end-to-end before training
2. Fill in all [BRACKETED PLACEHOLDERS] with facility-specific information
3. Pull up current evacuation map for Emergency Action Plan training
4. Confirm all emergency contact numbers are current
5. Prepare and print sign-in roster (IIPP-FORM-006)
6. File completed sign-in in employee personnel records (SB 513)

## Key Compliance Points

**Employer obligation:** Written IIPP required for EVERY California employer with 1+ employees. No size exemption.

**Cal/OSHA 300 Log:** Required for employers with 11+ employees in most industries.

**Annual 300A posting:** Sign and post every year from February 1 – April 30, even if zero incidents.

**Serious injury reporting:** Fatality, hospitalization, amputation, or eye loss → call Cal/OSHA at 1-800-963-9424 within **8 hours**.

**Heat illness (indoor expansion):** The 2024 Cal/OSHA amendment extended heat illness requirements to ANY indoor environment reaching 80°F — warehouses, kitchens, factories, laundries.

**SB 513 (2026):** Training records are now personnel records. File in employee personnel file; produce on request within 30 days.
