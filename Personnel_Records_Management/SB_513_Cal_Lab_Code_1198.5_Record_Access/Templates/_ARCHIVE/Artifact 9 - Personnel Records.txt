Artifact 9: Personnel Records Governance and Containerized Data Manual (2026)
Version: 6.0 (Deep Research Audit) Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Primary Mandate: Cal. Gov. Code § 12999(a)(3) & Cal. Lab. Code § 1198.5
1. Mandatory Data Segregation: The Six-Container System
Legal Authority: Cal. Gov. Code § 12999(a)(3); Cal. Lab. Code § 1198.5 Mandate: > "Any demographic information gathered by an employer or labor contractor... shall be collected and stored separately from employees' personnel records."
A. Technical Isolation of Record Containers
* Container 1: General Personnel History
   * [ ] Store offer letters, job applications, resumes, job descriptions, evaluations, and disciplinary notices.
* Container 2: Confidential Medical Records
   * [ ] Segregate doctor's notes, medical certifications, FMLA/CFRA documentation, and ADA interactive process notes into a locked, restricted-access file.
* Container 3: Immigration and I-9 Audit Protection
   * [ ] Maintain all Form I-9s and supporting identity documents in a separate repository to insulate from national origin discrimination claims.
* Container 4: Payroll and Timekeeping Logic
   * [ ] Store itemized wage statements, tax forms (W-4, DE-4), and daily time records (clock-in/out/meal periods).
* Container 5: Investigative and Emergency Data
   * [ ] Isolate harassment/discrimination complaint logs, legal notes, and mandatory SB 294 emergency arrest contacts.
* Container 6: SB 464 Demographic Repository
   * [ ] Store race, ethnicity, sex, and voluntary sexual orientation data used for CRD pay reporting; must be physically or digitally separated from main personnel files.
2. Enhanced Education and Training Records (SB 513)
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1198.5 (as amended by SB 513) Mandate: > "An employer who maintains education or training records shall ensure those records include... name of the employee... provider... duration and date... core competencies... and resulting certification."
A. Performance Metadata Integration
* Mandatory Content Fields
   * [ ] Employee Name: Record full legal name on every log.
   * [ ] Provider: Explicitly list the trainer or training organization.
   * [ ] Temporal Data: Document specific date and total duration (hours/minutes).
   * [ ] Core Competencies: Detail specific skills (e.g., "Software Proficiency," "Forklift Operation").
   * [ ] Certification: Store copies of certificates, licenses, or qualifications.
B. Regulatory Retention
* Standards
   * [ ] Post-Termination: Maintain SB 513 records for at least 3 years after termination.
   * [ ] Audit: Ensure "toolbox talks" and harassment training meet these metadata standards.
3. Right of Inspection and Request Fulfillment
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1198.5(b) Mandate: > "Make the contents... available... not later than 30 calendar days from the date the employer receives a written request."
A. Request Processing Protocols
* Timelines
   * [ ] 30-Day Fulfillment: Produce records within 30 days (extension to 35 only by mutual written agreement).
   * [ ] Former Employees: Limit to one request per year for former staff.
* Privacy Controls
   * [ ] Redactions: Redact names of non-supervisory employees to protect coworker privacy.
   * [ ] Litigation Stay: Suspend inspection rights if employee has a pending lawsuit related to personnel matters.
