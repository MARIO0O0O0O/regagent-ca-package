California Recruitment & Hiring Compliance Checklist (2026)
Version: 1.0 Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Primary Mandates: FEHA, Ban the Box, Salary History Ban, AB 2188 (Cannabis).
1. Job Postings & Applications
Before you even interview, ensure compliance.
* [ ] Pay Transparency: Must include the Pay Scale (Salary Range) in ALL job postings (if 15+ employees).
* [ ] No Discriminatory Language: Avoid terms like "young/energetic" (Ageism) or "digital native."
* [ ] "Ban the Box" (Initial App): You CANNOT ask about criminal history on the initial job application.
   * [ ] Action: Remove checkboxes asking "Have you ever been convicted of a felony?"
* [ ] Salary History Ban: You CANNOT ask an applicant about their current or prior salary/benefits.
   * [ ] Permitted: You can ask "What are your salary expectations?"
2. The Interview Phase
What you can and cannot ask.
* [ ] Prohibited Topics:
   * [ ] Salary History.
   * [ ] Criminal History (until after conditional offer).
   * [ ] Age, Marital Status, Children, Religion.
   * [ ] Prior Cannabis Use (SB 700).
* [ ] Social Media: You CANNOT ask candidates for their social media passwords or to access their private accounts (Lab Code § 980).
3. Background Checks (The "Fair Chance" Process)
Strict sequencing is required. You cannot run the check until you make a job offer.
1. [ ] Conditional Offer: Make a job offer in writing, contingent on a background check.
2. [ ] Disclosure & Authorization: Provide a standalone disclosure form (ICRAA/FCRA compliant) and get written authorization.
3. [ ] The Check: Run the background check.
4. [ ] Adverse Action (If hit found):
   * [ ] Individualized Assessment: You cannot auto-reject. You must consider: Nature of offense, time passed, and relevance to the job.
   * [ ] Pre-Adverse Action Notice: Send notice + copy of report + right to respond (wait 5 business days).
   * [ ] Final Decision: If still rejecting, send Final Adverse Action Notice.
4. Drug Testing (AB 2188 / SB 700)
The rules changed significantly in 2024/2025.
* [ ] Pre-Employment Testing:
   * [ ] You CANNOT test for non-psychoactive cannabis metabolites (urine/hair tests are mostly obsolete for this).
   * [ ] You MUST use oral swab tests that detect active THC (impairment) if you test for weed at all.
   * [ ] Exception: Building trades and federal positions are exempt.
* [ ] Inquiry Ban: You cannot ask "Have you used marijuana in the past?"
5. CROSS-REFERENCE: HOW OTHER LAWS IMPACT HIRING
5.1 PAY DATA → HIRING
Legal Principle: Pay Scale Disclosure. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] External Posts: Ensure Indeed/LinkedIn posts have the range.
* [ ] Recordkeeping: Keep records of job descriptions and wage ranges for 3 years. Cross-Reference: → [Pay Data Checklist]
5.2 IMMIGRATION → HIRING
Legal Principle: Unfair Documentary Practices. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Timing: Do NOT ask for I-9 documents during the interview. Only ask after hire (Day 1-3).
* [ ] Choice: Never specify "Bring your Green Card." Ask for "List A or List B+C documents." Cross-Reference: → [Immigration Checklist]
5.3 AB 692 (STAY-OR-PAY) → HIRING
Legal Principle: Offer Letters. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Template Audit: Ensure offer letters do not contain illegal "training repayment" clauses. Cross-Reference: → [AB 692 Stay-or-Pay Checklist]
6. Penalties
* Fair Chance Act: Fines per violation + Lawsuits.
* Salary History: Misdemeanor + Civil penalties.
* FCRA/ICRAA: Class action lawsuits for improper background check forms (e.g., burying the auth in the application).
