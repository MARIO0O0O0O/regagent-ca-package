California Lactation Accommodation Checklist (2026)
Version: 5.0 (Toolkit Upgrade) Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Primary Mandate: Cal. Lab. Code § 1030-1034
1. Mandatory Lactation Facility Standards
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1031 Mandate: > "A lactation room or location shall not be a bathroom and shall be in close proximity to the employee's work area... [and] contain a surface to place a breast pump and personal items... [and] a place to sit."
A. Location and Privacy Requirements
* Physical Space
   * Bathroom Prohibition
      * [ ] Ensure space is entirely separate from any bathroom, toilet stall, or locker room with toilets.
   * Privacy
      * [ ] Equip room with functional lock or "In Use" signage to prevent intrusion/view.
   * Proximity
      * [ ] Audit travel distance to ensure "close proximity" to workspace.
   * Priority
      * [ ] If multi-purpose, implement policy giving lactation absolute precedence.
B. Functional Amenities and Sanitation
* Equipment Access
   * Power/Surface
      * [ ] Provide electrical outlets or stable alternative power (cords/charging stations).
      * [ ] Ensure clean, flat surface for pump/supplies.
      * [ ] Provide at least one functional, clean chair.
   * Sanitation
      * [ ] Provide sink with clean running water (not a restroom sink) in close proximity.
      * [ ] Provide refrigerator or employer-provided cooling device (high-quality cooler) for milk storage.
      * [ ] Verify room is free of hazardous materials (Lab Code § 6382).
2. Break Time and Operational Policies
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1030 Mandate: > "Provide a reasonable amount of break time... each time the employee has need to express milk."
A. Break Administration
* Scheduling
   * Frequency
      * [ ] Allow breaks "each time" needed without daily caps.
   * Compensation
      * [ ] Run concurrently with paid rest breaks where possible (paid); extra time is unpaid but protected.
   * Documentation
      * [ ] Prohibit managers from requesting doctor's notes to justify lactation frequency.
B. Request Process
* Employer Duty
   * Interactive Dialogue
      * [ ] Provide formal written response if a specific space/time request cannot be met.
      * [ ] If <50 employees and claiming "undue hardship," document specific difficulty but provide non-bathroom alternative regardless.
3. Mandatory Policy Distribution
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1034 Mandate: > "Distribute the policy... to new employees upon hiring and when an employee... requests parental leave."
A. Distribution Channels
* Triggers
   * Onboarding
      * [ ] Include full policy in 2026 orientation materials for all new hires.
   * Leave
      * [ ] Deliver policy upon any inquiry regarding PDL or CFRA.
   * Handbook
      * [ ] Integrate permanently into Employee Handbook.
4. Enforcement and Anti-Retaliation
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1033 Mandate: > "Denial... shall be deemed a failure to comply for purposes of Section 226.7... [and] employer shall not... retaliate."
A. Penalty Mitigation
* Consequences
   * Premium Pay
      * [ ] Pay 1 hour regular wage for each day a compliant break/space is denied.
   * Civil Fines
      * [ ] Avoid $100 daily civil penalty for non-compliance.
* Training
   * Anti-Retaliation
      * [ ] Explicitly prohibit "productivity" discipline related to lactation breaks.
      * [ ] Include lactation status as protected category in harassment training.
