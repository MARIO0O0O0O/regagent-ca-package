🛡️ SECTION 2: SB 553 TEMPLATE (Violence Prevention)
Document Title: Model Workplace Violence Prevention Plan (WVPP) Statute: CA Labor Code § 6401.9 Effective: July 1, 2024 (Enforced 2026)
[COMPANY NAME] WORKPLACE VIOLENCE PREVENTION PLAN
1. RESPONSIBILITY The implementation of this WVPP is the responsibility of [Safety Officer Name/Title]. All employees have the authority to report concerns without fear of reprisal.
2. COMPLIANCE PROCEDURES
* Reporting: Employees must report threats/incidents to [HR/Manager] immediately.
* Emergency: In imminent danger, employees are authorized to call 9-1-1 immediately.
* Log: A "Violent Incident Log" (redacted of personal names) will be maintained for 5 years.
3. HAZARD ASSESSMENT We assess the following risks specific to our LA operations:
* [] Exchange of money with the public.
* [] Working alone or in isolated areas.
* [] Providing services to volatile customers.
4. TRAINING Annual training will cover:
* How to obtain a copy of this plan.
* How to report incidents.
* Strategies to avoid physical confrontation.
5. POST-INCIDENT RESPONSE We will provide immediate medical care, conduct a debriefing session, and review this plan for effectiveness within 7 days of any incident.
📢 SECTION 3: SB 294 NOTICE (Annual Requirement)
Document Title: 2026 "Know Your Rights" Annual Notice Deadline: Feb 1, 2026 (and annually thereafter) Distribution: Must be stand-alone email or hard copy.
NOTICE TO EMPLOYEES: YOUR RIGHTS UNDER CALIFORNIA LAW
In accordance with the Workplace Know Your Rights Act (SB 294), [Company Name] is providing this summary of your protections:
1. Workers' Compensation: You have the right to medical care and disability payments for work-related injuries.
2. Immigration Rights: You have the right to notice of any I-9 inspections. We will not engage in unfair immigration-related practices.
3. Organizing: You have the right to discuss wages, working conditions, and unionization without retaliation.
4. Police Interaction: You retain constitutional rights (4th/5th Amendment) during any law enforcement interaction at the workplace.
EMERGENCY CONTACT DESIGNATION: You have the right to designate a contact person. If you are detained or arrested at work, we will notify this person on your behalf. Please update your designation in the Employee Portal by March 30, 2026.
