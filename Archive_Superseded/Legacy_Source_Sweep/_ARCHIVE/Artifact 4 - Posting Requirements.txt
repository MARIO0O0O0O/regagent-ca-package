California Posting Requirements Checklist (2026)
Version: 6.0 (User Provided Update) Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Primary Mandate: Cal. Lab. Code § 1182.12 & IWC Wage Orders
1. Mandatory Wage and Hour Postings
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1182.12 Mandate: > "Effective January 1, 2026, the minimum wage is $16.90 per hour for all employers, not otherwise covered by a higher minimum wage specific to an industry or a locality."
A. State and Local Minimum Wage Rates
* Universal Rate Compliance
   * [ ] State Minimum Wage Poster (MW-2026): Display the official 2026 notice reflecting the $16.90 per hour rate for all employers regardless of size.
   * [ ] Exempt Salary Adjustment: Verify that employees classified as exempt are paid the 2026 minimum salary of $70,304 annually.
* Special Industry and Local Rates
   * [ ] Fast Food Restaurant Supplement: If applicable, post the supplement for fast food employees receiving at least $20.00 per hour.
   * [ ] Healthcare Worker Minimum Wage: Post the specific industry rate for healthcare facilities which may exceed the state minimum.
   * [ ] Local Ordinance Postings: In jurisdictions with higher local rates, display municipal posters alongside the state notice as the stricter standard prevails.
B. IWC Wage Orders and Administrative Notices
* Industry-Specific Orders
   * [ ] Applicable IWC Wage Order: Post the full text of the Wage Order relevant to the business industry (e.g., Order 4 for Professional/Clerical); summary versions are insufficient.
* Payday and Gratuity Protection
   * [ ] Payday Notice (DLSE 8): Post a completed notice specifying the regular paydays and the time/place of payment.
   * [ ] Tip Protection Compliance: Post notices clarifying that employers are prohibited from collecting or taking any portion of tips left for employees.
2. Health, Safety, and Protection Postings
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1102.8 & § 6328 Mandate: > "Must be prominently displayed in lettering larger than size 14 type and include a list of employee rights and responsibilities under the whistleblower laws, including the telephone number of the whistleblower hotline maintained by the office of the California Attorney General."
A. Whistleblower and Rights Education
* Transparency and Hotline Access
   * [ ] Whistleblower Protection Notice: Ensure the notice is printed in lettering larger than size 14 type and includes the Attorney General’s whistleblower hotline.
B. Cal/OSHA Safety Standards
* Workplace Safety Notifications
   * [ ] Safety & Health Protection on the Job: Display the mandatory Cal/OSHA notice (available in English and Spanish).
   * [ ] Emergency Phone Number List (S-500): Post a list of emergency responders, including local fire, police, and hospital contacts.
   * [ ] Annual Summary of Injuries (Form 300A): Post the summary of occupational injuries/illnesses from February 1 through April 30 each year.
* Hazardous Substances and Access
   * [ ] Access to Medical and Exposure Records: Provide information regarding rights to access safety data sheets and exposure records for hazardous substances.
3. Civil Rights, Leave, and Insurance Benefits
Legal Authority: Cal. Gov. Code § 12950 & Unemp. Ins. Code § 1089 Mandate: > "California law prohibits workplace discrimination and harassment... and the latest information can be obtained from the California Civil Rights Department (CRD)."
A. Anti-Discrimination and Inclusion
* FEHA Mandatory Disclosures
   * [ ] Workplace Discrimination and Harassment (CRD-E07P): Post the updated version of the discrimination prohibition notice (e.g., CRD-E07P-ENG).
   * [ ] Transgender Rights in the Workplace (CRD-E04P): Display the mandatory notice regarding transgender rights in a prominent location.
B. Leave Entitlements and Insurance
* Paid Leave and Victim Rights
   * [ ] Paid Sick Leave (Healthy Workplaces/Healthy Families Act): Display the 2026 notice, which includes expanded usage for crime-related absences.
   * [ ] Family Care & Medical Leave (CRD-100-21): For employers with 5 or more employees, post rights under CFRA and Pregnancy Disability Leave (PDL).
* Insurance and Workers' Compensation
   * [ ] Notice to Employees: UI/DI/PFL (DE 1857A): Advise employees of benefits for Unemployment, Disability, and Paid Family Leave insurance.
   * [ ] Workers' Compensation Poster: Post the notice including the claims administrator's contact information and insurer details.
4. Federal Mandatory Postings
Legal Authority: 29 CFR § 516.4 Mandate: > "Every employer of employees subject to the Act's minimum wage provisions... shall post and keep posted a notice explaining the Act..."
A. Consolidated Federal Standards
* Wage and Equal Opportunity
   * [ ] FLSA Minimum Wage (WHD-1088): Display the federal minimum wage notice.
   * [ ] Know Your Rights (EEOC-P/E-1): Post the consolidated federal notice regarding non-discrimination.
   * [ ] Employee Polygraph Protection Act (EPPA): Post the mandatory federal notice regarding polygraph testing.
* Family and Medical Leave (Federal)
   * [ ] FMLA Notice (WH 1420): Mandatory for employers with 50 or more employees; post the federal FMLA rights summary.
