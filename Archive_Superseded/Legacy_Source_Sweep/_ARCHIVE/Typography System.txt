HRbiz.org Typography System
Primary Typeface: Montserrat
Usage: Headings, Navigation, Buttons Why: Montserrat is geometric and highly legible, conveying modern authority and clarity suited for municipal and compliance contexts.
* H1 (Main Titles): Montserrat Bold (700), 32px / 40px line-height
* H2 (Section Titles): Montserrat SemiBold (600), 24px / 32px line-height
* H3 (Subsections): Montserrat Medium (500), 20px / 28px line-height
* H4-H6: Montserrat Medium (500), 16px / 24px line-height
Secondary Typeface: Open Sans
Usage: Body Copy, Long-form Reports, Email Correspondence Why: Open Sans is neutral, friendly, and optimized for print and web reading. It balances the "academic" background with small business accessibility.
* Body Text: Open Sans Regular (400), 16px / 1.6 line-height
* Bold Text: Open Sans Bold (700)
* Captions/Small: Open Sans Regular, 14px / 1.5 line-height
Accent Typeface: Merriweather
Usage: Pull quotes, "Fact-Finding" summaries, Formal Letterhead details Why: A serif font adds a touch of academic rigor and legal professionalism.
Web-Safe Fallbacks
* Headings: Verdana, Geneva, sans-serif
* Body: Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif
* Accent: Georgia, serif
Hierarchy Example
H1: Employee Misconduct Investigation Protocol
H2: Fact-Finding Phase
During the initial assessment (Body text in Open Sans), the investigator must remain impartial. As stated in the Employee Handbook (Accent in Merriweather), all parties have a right to be heard.
