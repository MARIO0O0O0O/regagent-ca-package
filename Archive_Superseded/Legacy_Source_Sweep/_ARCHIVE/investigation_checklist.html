PHASE 3: FINDINGS & DETERMINATION

Credibility Assessment

Evaluate each party using the following factors:
☐ Inherent Plausibility
○ Are accounts internally consistent and logical?
○ Do accounts make sense given the context?
☐ Corroboration
○ Are accounts supported by witnesses?
○ Are accounts supported by documents or physical evidence?
○ Do independent sources confirm key facts?
☐ Motive to Lie or Bias
○ Does any party have reason to be untruthful?
○ Are there conflicts of interest or biases?
○ Is there evidence of personal animosity?
☐ Perception and Opportunity
○ Could the person reasonably perceive what they describe?
○ Were they in position to observe/experience the conduct?
○ Were there barriers to perception (distance, time, obstructions)?
☐ Consistency Over Time
○ Are statements consistent across multiple interviews?
○ Do early statements match later statements?
○ Are there material inconsistencies that undermine credibility?
☐ Demeanor (used cautiously)
○ Does the person appear candid and forthcoming?
○ Note: Demeanor alone is NOT determinative
☐ Credibility assessments documented for all parties

Standard of Proof

Preponderance of the Evidence: Is it more likely than not (>50%) that the alleged conduct occurred?
☐ Evidence weighed using preponderance standard
☐ All evidence considered (not just complainant vs. respondent)
☐ Conflicts in evidence resolved

Findings

☐ Determination Made:
☐ SUSTAINED: Sufficient evidence that conduct occurred and violated policy
☐ NOT SUSTAINED: Insufficient evidence to determine (inconclusive)
☐ UNFOUNDED: Evidence shows conduct did not occur
☐ If sustained, specific policy violations identified
☐ If sustained, severity assessed (severe, moderate, minor)
☐ Findings support conclusion (evidence-based, not speculation)
