Harassment Investigation Process

Sapphos Environmental Inc. - 6-Phase Investigation Framework
Commitment: Prompt, Fair, Thorough, and Impartial Investigations
Phase 1
Complaint Intake (Day 0-1)
‚ñº
  • Complaint received by EEO Officer/HR
  • Document complaint details using standardized intake form
  • Assess severity and immediate risks to employee safety
  • Implement interim protective measures if needed (separation, no-contact directive, interim leave)
  • Notify complainant investigation will proceed and retaliation is prohibited
Phase 2
Investigation (Days 2-[Timeline])
‚ñº
  • Assign qualified investigator (external attorney or licensed PI recommended for impartiality)
  • Preserve and gather evidence (emails, text messages, documents, calendar records, security footage)
  • Interview complainant: Obtain detailed chronological account with dates, witnesses, and impact
  • Interview respondent: Present allegations and provide opportunity for full response (due process)
  • Interview witnesses: Gather corroborating or contradicting testimony from all relevant parties
  • Conduct site visits if applicable to assess visibility and opportunity for alleged conduct
  • Assess credibility of all parties using CRD-approved factors (plausibility, corroboration, motive, consistency)
Phase 3
Findings & Determination
‚ñº
  • Apply preponderance of evidence standard: "More likely than not" (51% certainty required)
  • Determine finding for each allegation: Sustained, Not Sustained, or Unfounded
  • Document findings in comprehensive written investigation report (9-element structure)
  • Include detailed credibility assessments explaining rationale for determinations
  • Recommend remedial action if policy violations found (discipline, training, separation, monitoring)
Phase 4
Remedial Action
‚ñº
  • Implement disciplinary action if warranted (warning, suspension, demotion, termination based on severity)
  • Separate parties if necessary through reassignment, schedule changes, or transfer (avoid burdening complainant)
  • Restore denied opportunities to victim (promotions, projects, raises lost due to harassment)
  • Provide mandatory training if appropriate (harassment prevention refresher, professionalism, diversity)
  • Update policies and procedures if investigation revealed gaps or systemic issues
Phase 5
Notification
‚ñº
  • Notify complainant of general outcome (sustained/not sustained/unfounded) and that action has been taken
  • Notify respondent of specific findings and disciplinary action being imposed
  • Remind both parties: Retaliation is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate discipline
  • Maintain confidentiality of investigation details and specific discipline (respondent's discipline is confidential)
Phase 6
Post-Investigation Monitoring
‚ñº
  • Monitor for retaliation: Check in with complainant at 1 week, 2 weeks, 30 days, and 60 days
  • Follow up with complainant to ensure harassment has stopped and work environment is appropriate
  • Assess effectiveness of remedial measures: Is discipline having intended effect? Any ongoing concerns?
  • Document all follow-up actions and conversations in Litigation/Investigations File
  • Respond immediately to any new issues: If retaliation or continued harassment occurs, investigate and escalate discipline
