MANDATORY REPORTING DUTY
✓ I have a mandatory duty to report any harassment, discrimination, or retaliation that I:
• Witness directly – any conduct I personally observe
• Am told about by an employee – even if the employee asks me to keep it confidential
• Become aware of through any means – rumors, secondhand information, or any other source
✓ I must report immediately (same business day if possible) to the EEO Officer at [PLACEHOLDER: EEO_OFFICER_CONTACT]. Delayed reporting may put the Company and me at legal risk.
✓ I CANNOT promise confidentiality to an employee who reports harassment to me. If an employee asks me to keep a complaint confidential, I must explain:
"I understand this is difficult, but I am required by Company policy to report this to our EEO Officer. The investigation will be handled as confidentially as possible, but I cannot promise complete confidentiality because the Company has a duty to investigate."
FAILURE TO REPORT IS A VIOLATION OF COMPANY POLICY and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of my employment.
✓ Under California law (Gov. Code § 12940(j)), I can be held personally liable for harassment. This means I could be sued individually and held responsible for damages, separate from any liability the Company faces.
✓ I am held to a higher standard of conduct as a supervisor/manager and have a responsibility to:
• Model respectful and professional behavior at all times
• Create an inclusive and harassment-free work environment for my team
• Address inappropriate conduct promptly before it escalates to harassment
• Support employees who report harassment and ensure they are not retaliated against
• Take all complaints seriously, even if I personally believe they lack merit
• Never discourage an employee from reporting or minimize their concerns
✓ I have completed (or will complete within 6 months of my supervisory appointment) the required 2-hour supervisory harassment prevention training that covers:
• California and federal anti-harassment laws
• My mandatory duty to report
• How to prevent harassment on my team
• How to respond when an employee reports harassment
• My potential personal liability under California law
✓ I understand my responsibilities and will fulfill them. I recognize that my role as a supervisor carries additional legal obligations and that failure to meet these obligations can result in serious consequences for employees, the Company, and myself personally.
✓ I will not retaliate or allow retaliation against any employee who reports harassment, participates in an investigation, or opposes unlawful practices.
✓ If I have any questions about my reporting obligations or how to handle a harassment complaint, I will immediately contact the EEO Officer at [PLACEHOLDER: EEO_OFFICER_CONTACT] or the HR Director before taking any action.
