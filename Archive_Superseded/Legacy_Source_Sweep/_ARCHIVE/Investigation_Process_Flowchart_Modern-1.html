Phase 1: Complaint Intake & Triage

Day 0-1
‚ñº
1
Receive Complaint
Complaint received by EEO Officer, HR Director, Legal Counsel, or any supervisor/manager. May be verbal or written. Listen carefully, take detailed notes, and avoid making promises or judgments.
IMMEDIATE ACTION
2
Document Complaint Details
Use standardized intake form to capture: complainant/respondent information, dates/times/locations, description of conduct, witnesses, evidence, and impact on complainant.
SAME DAY
3
Assess Severity & Risk
Evaluate immediate safety threats, severity of allegations (physical conduct, quid pro quo, pattern), risk to other employees, and potential for ongoing harassment.
RISK ASSESSMENT
4
Implement Interim Measures
If necessary, separate parties (reassignment, schedule change), issue no-contact directive, place respondent on interim leave, or implement monitoring. Avoid burdening complainant.
IF WARRANTED PROTECT COMPLAINANT
5
Notify Complainant
Inform complainant that investigation will proceed, explain timeline and process, assure confidentiality to extent possible, emphasize retaliation protections, and provide EEO Officer contact.
NEXT BUSINESS DAY

Phase 2: Investigation Planning

Day 1-2
‚ñº
1
Designate Investigator
Assign qualified investigator (external attorney or licensed PI recommended for impartiality). Ensure no conflict of interest. Document rationale for selection.
QUALIFIED PERSONNEL EXTERNAL PREFERRED
2
Create Witness List
Identify all potential witnesses: direct witnesses, indirect witnesses (told about conduct), character witnesses, pattern witnesses, and supervisors of both parties.
3
Plan Interview Sequence
Standard order: (1) Complainant, (2) Respondent, (3) Direct witnesses, (4) Indirect witnesses, (5) Follow-up as needed. Schedule in neutral locations with sufficient time.
4
Identify Evidence
List all evidence to collect: emails, instant messages, texts, documents, calendar records, security footage, photos, performance reviews, prior complaints, training records.
5
Preserve Evidence (IT Hold)
CRITICAL: Contact IT immediately to place litigation hold on all electronic records for complainant, respondent, and key witnesses. Prevent automatic deletion. Create forensic backups.
IMMEDIATE - WITHIN 24 HOURS

Phase 3: Investigation Execution

Day 2-[Timeline]
‚ñº
1
Interview Complainant
Obtain detailed chronological account using open-ended questions. Cover: background, each incident (dates/times/locations), witnesses, evidence, impact. Take verbatim notes. Provide opportunity for questions.
FIRST INTERVIEW DETAILED DOCUMENTATION
2
Interview Respondent
Present allegations with sufficient detail for meaningful response (due process). Allow full opportunity to respond, provide alternative account, identify witnesses/evidence. Warn against retaliation.
DUE PROCESS REQUIRED
3
Interview Witnesses
Interview separately to avoid contamination. Use open-ended questions. Assess: perception ability (could they see/hear?), memory, bias, consistency. Document all testimony.
4
Review Documents
Analyze all collected evidence for corroboration or contradiction. Look for patterns, timestamps alignment, tone/content of communications, gaps or inconsistencies.
5
Site Visit (If Applicable)
Visit location where conduct allegedly occurred. Assess: visibility (could others see?), opportunity (is conduct plausible here?), layout. Document with photos/measurements.

Phase 4: Credibility Assessment & Findings

Days [Timeline-2] to [Timeline]
‚ñº
1
Apply CRD Credibility Factors
Evaluate all testimony using 8 CRD-approved factors: (1) Inherent plausibility, (2) Motive to lie, (3) Corroboration, (4) Perception ability, (5) History of honesty, (6) Habit/pattern, (7) Consistency, (8) Demeanor (use cautiously).
CRD FRAMEWORK
2
Apply Preponderance Standard
Determine for each allegation: Is it more likely than not (51%+ certainty) that the conduct occurred? This is workplace standard, NOT "beyond reasonable doubt."
LEGAL STANDARD
3
Reach Determination
For each allegation, determine: SUSTAINED (violations found), NOT SUSTAINED (insufficient evidence/inconclusive), or UNFOUNDED (evidence shows did not occur).
4
Document in Investigation Report
Prepare comprehensive written report with 9 elements: investigator details, case overview, objective, steps taken, evidence summary, credibility assessment, findings, policy violations, recommendations.
REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

Phase 5: Remedial Action & Notification

Days [Timeline] to [Timeline+2]
‚ñº
1
Determine Appropriate Discipline
Consider: severity of conduct, position of harasser (supervisors held to higher standard), impact on victim, prior complaints, pervasiveness. Discipline ranges from warning to termination. Consult Legal Counsel before imposing.
LEGAL COUNSEL REVIEW
2
Implement Corrective Action
Execute discipline, separate parties if needed, restore denied opportunities to victim, provide mandatory training, issue no-contact directive, implement monitoring. Document all actions taken.
3
Notify Complainant
Inform of general outcome (sustained/not sustained/unfounded) and that action has been taken. DO NOT disclose specific discipline of respondent (confidential). Emphasize retaliation protections. Provide follow-up contact.
4
Notify Respondent
Present findings, policy violations, and specific discipline being imposed. Set behavioral expectations. Warn against retaliation. If termination, coordinate final paycheck, COBRA, property return, IT access termination.

Phase 6: Post-Investigation Monitoring

30-60+ Days
‚ñº
1
Structured Follow-Up Schedule
Check in with complainant at: 1 week, 2 weeks, 30 days, and 60 days. Ask about any retaliation, ongoing concerns, or effectiveness of remedies. Document all conversations.
SYSTEMATIC MONITORING
2
Monitor for Retaliation
Watch for red flags: sudden negative performance review, undesirable reassignment, increased scrutiny, hostile treatment, exclusion from opportunities, discipline for minor issues, spreading of rumors.
ZERO TOLERANCE
3
Assess Effectiveness
Evaluate whether corrective action achieved intended result. Has harassment stopped? Is respondent complying with behavioral expectations? Any new complaints against respondent?
4
Document Follow-Up
Maintain detailed records of all follow-up activities, conversations with complainant, supervisor observations, and any new issues. Store in Litigation/Investigations File.
5
Close Monitoring Phase
After 60+ days with no concerns, formally close monitoring phase. Document closure date and rationale. Remind complainant they can always report future concerns.
