1.1 Comprehensive Harassment Prevention Policy
Written policy prohibiting discrimination, harassment (including sexual harassment), and retaliation covering all employees, applicants, contractors, and third parties. Must be distributed to all employees.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12950(a) - "Every employer shall take all reasonable steps to prevent discrimination and harassment from occurring."

üìú Additional Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a) - "Every employer shall establish, publicize, and implement a procedure to investigate harassment complaints that includes: (1) A complaint process that provides a variety of avenues of complaint..."

REQUIRED - All Employers Immediate Implementation
1.2 Policy Must Include All Protected Characteristics
Policy must explicitly list all protected characteristics under FEHA including 2024-2025 legislative additions: reproductive health decision-making (SB 523), reproductive loss (SB 848), off-duty cannabis use (AB 2188), and criminal history protections.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12940(a) - Lists all protected bases: "race, religious creed, color, national origin, ancestry, physical disability, mental disability, medical condition, genetic information, marital status, sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression, age, sexual orientation, or military and veteran status"

üìú 2024-2025 Additions:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12945.8 (SB 523) - Reproductive health decision-making protections

Gov. Code ¬ß 12945.6 (SB 848) - Reproductive loss leave protections

Gov. Code ¬ß 12954 (AB 2188) - Off-duty cannabis use protections

REQUIRED - FEHA Compliance Update by January 2026
1.3 Multiple Internal Reporting Channels (No Chain of Command)
Policy must provide multiple avenues for reporting harassment without requiring employees to report to immediate supervisor. Must include at least 2-3 reporting options (EEO Officer, HR, Legal Counsel, any manager).
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(A) - "A complaint process that provides a variety of avenues of complaint...and does not require an employee to complain directly to the employee's immediate supervisor."

üìú CRD Guidance:

CRD Harassment Prevention Guide (2025) - "Employees should have multiple reporting options and should not be required to report to their harasser."

REQUIRED - 2 CCR ¬ß 11023
1.4 External Agency Filing Information (CRD & EEOC)
Policy must inform employees of their right to file complaints with California Civil Rights Department (CRD) and EEOC, including contact information and filing deadlines (3 years for CRD, 300 days for EEOC).
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(D) - "A statement that the employee has the right to file a complaint or charge with the Department of Fair Employment and Housing [now CRD]."

üìú Filing Deadlines:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12960(e)(5) - Extended CRD filing deadline to 3 years (effective January 1, 2020)

REQUIRED - 2 CCR ¬ß 11023
1.5 Investigation Process Description
Policy must describe the investigation process including timeline, confidentiality parameters, and commitment to prompt, fair, and thorough investigation.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(B) - "An impartial and timely investigation by qualified personnel."

üìú Employer Duty:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12940(k) - Employer must "take immediate and appropriate corrective action" when harassment is known or should have been known.

REQUIRED - 2 CCR ¬ß 11023
1.6 Retaliation Prohibitions (Explicit & Prominent)
Policy must include strong, explicit statement prohibiting retaliation against anyone who reports harassment, participates in investigation, or opposes unlawful practices. Must include examples of retaliation.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12940(h) - "For any employer...to discharge, expel, or otherwise discriminate against any person because the person has opposed any practices forbidden under this part or because the person has filed a complaint..."

üìú Additional Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(C) - "Assurance that the employer will protect the confidentiality of harassment complaints to the extent possible, and that the employer will take remedial measures independently of the complaint process, including interim separation of parties where appropriate."

REQUIRED - Gov. Code ¬ß 12940(h)
1.7 Supervisor Mandatory Reporting Duty
Policy must explicitly state that supervisors and managers have mandatory duty to report any harassment they witness, are told about, or become aware of. Failure to report is grounds for discipline.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12940(j)(3) - "An individual is personally liable for harassment if the individual is a supervisor with respect to the plaintiff, the individual personally committed the harassment, and the entity took reasonable steps to prevent and correct harassment."

üìú CRD Guidance:

CRD Harassment Prevention Guide (2025) - "Supervisors should be trained to report harassment even if the employee requests confidentiality."

REQUIRED - Supervisor Liability
1.8 Policy Applies to Virtual/Remote Work
Policy must explicitly cover harassment in virtual environments (email, video calls, instant messaging, collaboration platforms) and clarify that all protections apply to remote work.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11034(g) - "The prohibition of harassment on the basis of sex includes sexual harassment, gender harassment, and harassment based on pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions in any form of communication."

üìú CRD Guidance:

CRD Harassment Prevention Guide (2025) - "Harassment through electronic communications (email, text, social media) is covered. Employers should address harassment in virtual workplaces."

REQUIRED - 2025 CRD Update

Category 2: Training Requirements & Documentation

2.1 Supervisory Harassment Prevention Training (2 Hours, Every 2 Years)
All supervisory employees must receive 2 hours of sexual harassment and abusive conduct prevention training every 2 years. Training must be provided within 6 months of hire or promotion to supervisory role.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1(a) (AB 1825, SB 1343) - "An employer having 5 or more employees shall provide at least 2 hours of classroom or other effective interactive training and education regarding sexual harassment to all supervisory employees...and thereafter once every 2 years."

üìú Timeline:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1(b) - "The training and education required by subdivision (a) shall be completed by each supervisory employee...within 6 months of the assumption of a supervisory position."

REQUIRED - Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1 Deadline: January 1, 2027
2.2 Non-Supervisory Harassment Prevention Training (1 Hour, Every 2 Years)
All non-supervisory employees must receive 1 hour of sexual harassment and abusive conduct prevention training every 2 years. Training must be provided within 6 months of hire.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1(a) - "An employer having 5 or more employees shall provide at least 1 hour of classroom or other effective interactive training and education regarding sexual harassment to all nonsupervisory employees...and thereafter once every 2 years."

REQUIRED - Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1 Deadline: January 1, 2027
2.3 Temporary/Seasonal Employee Training (Within 30 Days or 100 Hours)
Temporary, seasonal, or short-term employees must receive harassment prevention training within 30 calendar days of hire OR within 100 hours worked, whichever comes first.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1(b) - "For an employee hired to work for less than 6 months, or a temporary or seasonal employee hired for a limited duration, the training required by subdivision (a) shall be provided within 30 calendar days after the hire date or within 100 hours worked, whichever occurs first."

REQUIRED - Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1
2.4 Training Must Cover Mandatory Content Elements
Training must include: (1) Definition of sexual harassment under FEHA; (2) Types of conduct that constitute harassment; (3) Remedies available to victims; (4) Strategies to prevent harassment; (5) Supervisors' obligation to report (supervisor training only); (6) Practical examples including harassment in virtual/online environments.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1(e) - Training content requirements including "information and practical guidance regarding the federal and state statutory provisions concerning the prohibition against and the prevention and correction of sexual harassment..."

üìú 2025 Update:

CRD Harassment Prevention Guide (2025) - Training must address harassment in virtual/remote work environments and online communications.

REQUIRED - Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1(e)
2.5 Training Must Be "Interactive"
Training must be interactive, not passive video or reading. Must allow questions and answers, use hypothetical scenarios, and provide opportunity for feedback.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1(c) - Training "may be completed by classroom training, web-based training, or other effective interactive training in compliance with this section."

üìú Definition of Interactive:

2 CCR ¬ß 11024 - Interactive training includes "a question-and-answer component" and "must be conducted by a trainer...or...provided online with certain requirements."

REQUIRED - Gov. Code ¬ß 12950.1(c)
2.6 Training Records & Certificates
Maintain training records including: employee name, training date, type of training (supervisory/non-supervisory), training provider, proof of completion. Records must be retained for minimum 2 years.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11024(e) - "Each employer shall keep records of the training provided, including the names of the employees trained, the date of training, the sign-in sheet, a copy of all certificates of attendance or completion issued, the type of training provided, a copy of all written or recorded materials provided..."

üìú Retention Period:

2 CCR ¬ß 11024(e) - Records must be retained "for a minimum of two years."

REQUIRED - 2 CCR ¬ß 11024 Retain 2+ Years
2.7 Training Attendance Roster
Create and maintain attendance roster for all training sessions documenting date, employee names, signatures (if in-person), and training type.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11024(e) - Employer must maintain "the sign-in sheet" and "a copy of all certificates of attendance or completion issued."

REQUIRED - 2 CCR ¬ß 11024 Retain 2+ Years
2.8 Training Compliance Tracker (Spreadsheet/Database)
Maintain spreadsheet or database tracking: employee name, hire date, position (supervisor/non-supervisor), last training date, next training due date, training completion status. Use to identify who needs training and avoid missed deadlines.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11024(e) - While not explicitly required, tracking system demonstrates compliance with record retention requirements and facilitates audit response.

3.1 Harassment Complaint Intake Form
Standardized form for documenting complaints including: complainant information, respondent information, dates/times/locations of alleged conduct, description of conduct, witnesses, evidence, impact on complainant. Ensures consistent documentation.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(A) - Requires "a complaint process" but does not mandate specific form. However, standardized intake form demonstrates systematic approach and facilitates investigation.

üìú Best Practice:

CRD provides sample intake form on website. Using official CRD form or creating custom form based on CRD template demonstrates good faith compliance.

BEST PRACTICE STRONGLY RECOMMENDED
3.2 Policy Acknowledgment Form (All Employees)
Form signed by each employee acknowledging receipt of harassment prevention policy, understanding of rights and reporting procedures, and understanding of retaliation protections.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a) - Requires employers to "establish, publicize, and implement" harassment prevention procedures. Signed acknowledgments demonstrate "publicize" requirement met.

üìú Litigation Defense:

Courts consider signed acknowledgments as evidence that employer fulfilled duty to communicate policy. See Trujillo v. North County Transit Dist. (1998) - Employee acknowledgment of receipt of harassment policy weighed in employer's favor.

STRONGLY RECOMMENDED Retain in Personnel File
3.3 Supervisor/Manager Acknowledgment Form (Special)
Enhanced acknowledgment form for supervisors emphasizing mandatory reporting duty, prohibition on promising confidentiality, and understanding of personal liability under California law.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12940(j)(3) - Supervisors face personal liability for harassment. Signed acknowledgment demonstrates supervisor was informed of mandatory reporting duty and personal liability risk.

STRONGLY RECOMMENDED Retain in Personnel File
3.4 Anonymous Reporting Mechanism (Optional but Recommended)
Provide mechanism for anonymous reporting such as dedicated email, online form, or third-party hotline. Reduces barrier to reporting for employees who fear retaliation.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(A) - Requires "variety of avenues of complaint" but does not mandate anonymous option. However, anonymous reporting removes barriers and demonstrates commitment to accessibility.

üìú CRD Guidance:

CRD Harassment Prevention Guide (2025) - "Employers should consider providing anonymous reporting options, though investigation may be limited without complainant cooperation."

4.1 Written Investigation Protocol/Procedures Manual
Documented investigation procedures outlining: intake process, investigator assignment, evidence preservation, interview protocols, credibility assessment, findings determination, remedial action, and notification procedures.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(B) - "An impartial and timely investigation by qualified personnel." Written protocols demonstrate systematic, impartial approach.

üìú Litigation Defense:

Courts evaluate whether employer had adequate investigation procedures in place. Written protocols show employer took reasonable steps to prevent/correct harassment. See Mathieu v. Norrell Corp. (2004).

STRONGLY RECOMMENDED
4.2 Investigation Process Flowchart (Visual Guide)
Visual flowchart showing 6-phase investigation process from complaint intake through post-investigation monitoring. Provides quick reference for investigators and demonstrates systematic approach.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(B) - Impartial and timely investigation required. Flowchart demonstrates structured, consistent process across all complaints.

BEST PRACTICE
4.3 Investigator's Checklist
Step-by-step checklist for investigators covering: evidence preservation, witness identification, interview preparation, credibility assessment factors, documentation requirements, and report writing.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(B) - Investigation must be "impartial and timely" and conducted "by qualified personnel." Checklist ensures thoroughness and consistency.

BEST PRACTICE
4.4 Interview Question Templates
Pre-prepared interview question templates for complainant interviews, respondent interviews, and witness interviews. Ensures consistent, thorough questioning while avoiding leading questions.
üìú Legal Authority:

CRD Harassment Prevention Guide (2025) - "Use open-ended questions that encourage narrative responses. Avoid leading questions that suggest desired answers."

BEST PRACTICE
4.5 Credibility Assessment Framework (CRD Factors)
Document outlining 8 CRD-approved credibility factors for evaluating witness testimony: inherent plausibility, motive to lie, corroboration, perception ability, history of honesty, habit/pattern, consistency, and demeanor (used cautiously).
üìú Legal Authority:

CRD Harassment Prevention Guide (2025) - Lists approved credibility factors for workplace investigations. Using CRD framework demonstrates alignment with state guidance.

üìú Preponderance Standard:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023 - Investigation determines whether harassment "more likely than not" occurred (preponderance of evidence standard, not "beyond reasonable doubt").

STRONGLY RECOMMENDED
4.6 Investigation Report Template
Standardized template for investigation reports including 9 required elements: investigator details, case overview, investigation objective, steps taken, evidence summary, credibility assessment, findings, policy violation analysis, and recommendations.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(B) - Investigation must be documented. Standardized report format ensures all required elements covered and creates audit trail.

üìú Attorney-Client Privilege:

If investigation conducted by external attorney, report should be addressed to Legal Counsel to maintain attorney-client privilege protection.

STRONGLY RECOMMENDED
4.7 Interim Protective Measures Protocol
Guidelines for implementing interim measures during investigation: separation of parties, no-contact directives, interim leave, schedule changes, monitoring. Must avoid burdening complainant.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(C) - "The employer will take remedial measures independently of the complaint process, including interim separation of parties where appropriate."

REQUIRED - 2 CCR ¬ß 11023
4.8 Evidence Preservation Protocol (IT Holds)
Procedures for immediately preserving electronic evidence upon complaint receipt: litigation hold on email accounts, preventing deletion of instant messages, preserving calendar records, securing security footage, documenting evidence chain of custody.
üìú Legal Authority:

Federal Rules of Civil Procedure Rule 37(e) - Spoliation sanctions for failure to preserve electronically stored information. State law parallel provisions apply. Must preserve evidence once litigation reasonably anticipated.

üìú Best Practice:

Implement IT hold within 24 hours of complaint receipt. Document all preservation efforts. Failure to preserve can result in adverse inference jury instruction in litigation.

STRONGLY RECOMMENDED
4.9 Discipline Matrix / Guidelines
Framework for determining appropriate discipline based on severity of conduct, position of harasser, prior complaints, impact on victim, and other factors. Ensures consistency and proportionality in discipline.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12940(k) - Employer must take "immediate and appropriate corrective action." Discipline must be "reasonably calculated to end the harassment."

üìú Supervisors:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12940(j)(3) - Supervisors held to higher standard. Same conduct warrants more severe discipline for supervisors than non-supervisors due to personal liability risk.

BEST PRACTICE
4.10 Post-Investigation Monitoring Protocol
Structured follow-up schedule after investigation closes: check-ins with complainant at 1 week, 2 weeks, 30 days, and 60 days. Monitor for retaliation, assess effectiveness of remedial measures, document all follow-up.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12940(k) - Employer's duty continues after investigation. Must ensure harassment stops and retaliation does not occur. Monitoring demonstrates ongoing commitment.

Category 5: Recordkeeping & File Management Systems

5.1 Separate Litigation/Investigations File
All harassment complaints, investigation records, findings, and discipline must be stored in file physically or electronically separate from personnel files. Access strictly limited to EEO Officer, HR Director, Legal Counsel, and authorized investigators.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(C) - "The employer will protect the confidentiality of harassment complaints to the extent possible." Separate file system demonstrates confidentiality protection.

üìú Privacy Protection:

California Constitution Article I, ¬ß 1 - Right to privacy. Mixing harassment investigation records with general personnel files can violate complainant privacy and create improper disclosure risk.

REQUIRED - Best Practice Retain 3+ Years Minimum
5.2 Investigation Records Retention (Minimum 3 Years)
Retain all investigation records for minimum 3 years from complaint date (matches CRD filing deadline). Longer retention if litigation hold applies. Records include: intake form, all interview notes, evidence, investigation report, notifications, follow-up documentation.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12960(e)(5) - CRD complaint filing deadline is 3 years from last incident. Employer must retain records at least as long as potential complaint period.

üìú Federal Standard:

29 CFR ¬ß 1602.14 - EEOC requires personnel records retention "for one year from the date of the making of the record or the personnel action involved, whichever occurs later." California 3-year standard is longer and controls.

REQUIRED Retain 3+ Years
5.3 Training Records Retention (Minimum 2 Years)
Retain training records (rosters, certificates, training materials) for minimum 2 years from training date. CRD may audit training compliance and request proof of training.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11024(e) - "Employers shall keep records of the training provided...for a minimum of two years and shall make such records available for inspection by the Department upon request."

REQUIRED - 2 CCR ¬ß 11024 Retain 2+ Years
5.4 Complaint Tracking Database/Spreadsheet
Centralized tracking system for all complaints documenting: complaint date, complainant (may use ID number for confidentiality), respondent, investigator, investigation completion date, finding, corrective action taken, follow-up dates, case status.
üìú Legal Authority:

While not explicitly mandated, tracking system facilitates compliance with 2 CCR ¬ß 11023 requirements for timely, documented investigation. Demonstrates systematic approach and allows trend analysis.

üìú Pattern Evidence:

Tracking multiple complaints against same respondent establishes pattern evidence, demonstrating employer knowledge and triggering heightened duty to act.

BEST PRACTICE
5.5 Digital/Physical Access Controls
Implement access restrictions on investigation files: digital folders with password protection and user permissions (only EEO Officer, HR Director, Legal Counsel); physical files in locked cabinet with restricted key access. Document who has access.
üìú Legal Authority:

2 CCR ¬ß 11023(a)(3)(C) - Employer must "protect the confidentiality of harassment complaints to the extent possible." Access controls demonstrate confidentiality measures.

üìú CMIA Compliance:

If harassment investigation involves medical information (mental health impact, medical treatment), Cal. Civ. Code ¬ß 56.20 (Confidentiality of Medical Information Act) may apply, requiring stricter confidentiality measures.

REQUIRED - Confidentiality
5.6 Annual Policy Review & Update Schedule
Schedule annual review of harassment prevention policy by HR Director, EEO Officer, and Legal Counsel. Update policy to reflect legislative changes, court decisions, and CRD guidance. Document review date and any changes made.
üìú Legal Authority:

Gov. Code ¬ß 12950(a) - Employer must take "all reasonable steps" to prevent harassment. Annual review demonstrates ongoing commitment and ensures policy remains current with law.

üìú Recent Updates:

2024-2025 legislative session added SB 523 (reproductive health), SB 848 (reproductive loss), AB 2188 (cannabis use) protections. Policies must be updated to include these new protected classes.
