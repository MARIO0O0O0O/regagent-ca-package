California Leave of Absence Checklist (1-49 Employees)
Version: 5.0 (Deep Research Audit) Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Primary Mandate: Cal. Gov. Code § 12945 (PDL) & 12945.2 (CFRA)
1. Pregnancy Disability Leave (PDL)
Legal Authority: Cal. Gov. Code § 12945 Mandate: > "Refuse to allow an employee disabled by pregnancy... to take a leave for a reasonable period of time not to exceed four months... The employee shall be entitled to utilize any accrued vacation leave."
A. Eligibility and Benefits
* Access
   * Day One
      * [ ] Verify PDL is available immediately upon hire (No tenure requirement).
      * [ ] Confirm coverage for employers with 5+ employees.
* Duration
   * Entitlement
      * [ ] Calculate max leave: 17.3 weeks (4 months) per pregnancy.
   * Benefits
      * [ ] Maintain employer-paid group health premiums for duration of leave.
2. California Family Rights Act (CFRA)
Legal Authority: Cal. Gov. Code § 12945.2 Mandate: > "Refuse to grant a request by any employee with more than 12 months of service... and at least 1,250 hours... to take up to 12 weeks of family care."
A. Small Employer Scope
* Thresholds
   * Applicability
      * [ ] Confirm CFRA applies to all worksites with 5+ employees.
   * Eligibility
      * [ ] Verify employee has 12 months service + 1,250 hours worked.
* Family Members
   * Coverage
      * [ ] Include "Designated Person" (blood/family equivalent association).
      * [ ] Include Child, Parent, Spouse, Domestic Partner, Grandparent, Grandchild, Sibling, Parent-in-law.
3. Paid Sick and Safe Leave (SB 616)
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 246 Mandate: > "Limit an employee's use of accrued paid sick days to 40 hours or five days in each year... 'full amount of leave' means five days or 40 hours."
A. Accrual and Usage
* Minimums
   * Usage
      * [ ] Implement 40-Hour / 5-Day annual usage floor (whichever provides more time).
   * Cap
      * [ ] Set accrual cap at 80 hours / 10 days minimum.
* Protected Use
   * Safe Time
      * [ ] Allow usage for judicial proceedings related to crime victimization (AB 2499).
4. Bereavement and Reproductive Loss
Legal Authority: Cal. Gov. Code § 12945.7 & 12945.6 Mandate: > "Unlawful... to refuse to grant a request by any employee to take up to five days of reproductive loss leave."
A. Operations
* Timelines
   * Eligibility
      * [ ] Verify 30-day employment tenure.
   * Completion
      * [ ] Ensure leave is completed within 3 months of event.
* Privacy
   * Documentation
      * [ ] Do NOT request documentation for Reproductive Loss events.
      * [ ] Request documentation for Bereavement (within 30 days) only if policy states.
5. Mandatory 2026 Postings
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1102.8 Mandate: > "Displayed in lettering larger than size 14-point type and include a list of employee rights... under the whistleblower laws."
A. Compliance
* Visuals
   * Whistleblower
      * [ ] Audit poster for 14-point font compliance.
   * Wage
      * [ ] Post $16.90 Minimum Wage notice.
