California Remote Work & Expense Reimbursement Checklist (2026)
Version: 5.0 (Toolkit Upgrade) Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Primary Mandate: Cal. Lab. Code § 2802
1. Mandatory Indemnification of Business Expenditures
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 2802 Mandate: > "An employer shall indemnify his or her employee for all necessary expenditures or losses incurred by the employee in direct consequence of the discharge of his or her duties... even though unlawful, unless the employee... believed them to be unlawful."
A. Communication and Digital Infrastructure
* Connectivity Costs
   * Personal Devices
      * [ ] Provide a "reasonable percentage" reimbursement for personal cell phones used for work calls, texts, or mandatory 2FA.
   * Data Plans
      * [ ] Pay the business portion of the bill even if the employee has an "unlimited" personal plan (paying for availability).
   * Internet
      * [ ] Reimburse a portion of high-speed internet costs if remote/hybrid work is directed or required.
B. Personal Vehicle and Transportation
* Mileage Standards
   * Rate
      * [ ] Apply the 2026 IRS standard rate of 72.5 cents per mile for all business travel.
   * Coverage
      * [ ] Ensure rate covers fuel, depreciation, maintenance, insurance, and registration.
   * Commute Rule
      * [ ] Clearly exclude home-to-office commuting; include travel between worksites or to clients.
C. Home Office Equipment and "Stay-or-Pay"
* Hardware & Ergonomics
   * Provision
      * [ ] Directly provide or fully reimburse for laptops, monitors, keyboards, and ergonomic chairs required for the role.
   * Software
      * [ ] Cover full cost of specialized software or subscriptions necessary for performance.
* Debt Ban (AB 692)
   * Prohibitions
      * [ ] Remove all clauses requiring repayment of relocation, training, or "quit fees" upon exit.
