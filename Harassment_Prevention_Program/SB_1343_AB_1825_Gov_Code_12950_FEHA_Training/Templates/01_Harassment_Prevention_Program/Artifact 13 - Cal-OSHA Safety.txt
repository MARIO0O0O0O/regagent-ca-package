California Cal/OSHA Safety Compliance & Training Audit (2026 Revision)
Version: 5.0 (Deep Research Audit) Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Primary Mandate: Cal. Lab. Code § 6401.7 & 6401.9
1. Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP)
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 6401.7 Mandate: > "Every employer shall establish, implement, and maintain an effective injury and illness prevention program... in writing and shall... include... [a] system for identifying and evaluating workplace hazards."
A. Program Administration
* Responsibility
   * Designation
      * [ ] Explicitly name the person/titles with authority for implementation.
   * Integration
      * [ ] Ensure IIPP is synchronized with the Workplace Violence Prevention Plan (WVPP).
B. Hazard Identification
* Inspections
   * Schedule
      * [ ] Conduct periodic inspections when plan established, new hazards introduced, or new processes start.
   * Reporting
      * [ ] Provide effective anonymous system for employees to report hazards without reprisal.
   * Correction
      * [ ] Establish written procedure to correct hazards in timely manner based on severity.
2. Workplace Violence Prevention Plan (WVPP)
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 6401.9 Mandate: > "The plan shall be in writing and shall be available and easily accessible to employees... at all times."
A. Incident Classification
* Types
   * Categorization
      * [ ] Type 1: Criminal Intent (No legitimate business).
      * [ ] Type 2: Customer/Client violence.
      * [ ] Type 3: Worker-on-Worker violence.
      * [ ] Type 4: Personal Relationship spillover.
B. Violent Incident Log
* Logging Standards
   * Privacy
      * [ ] Maintain anonymized log based on investigations.
      * [ ] Omit PII: Ensure NO names or SSNs of involved parties appear in the log.
   * Fields
      * [ ] Include date, time, location, detailed description, and consequences.
3. Heat Illness Prevention (Indoor & Outdoor)
Legal Authority: 8 CCR § 3395 (Outdoor) & § 3396 (Indoor) Mandate: > "Applies to all indoor work areas where the temperature equals or exceeds 82 degrees Fahrenheit... The water shall be located as close as practicable."
A. Environmental Triggers
* Proximity
   * Water
      * [ ] Place water stations "As Close as Practicable" (avoid obstacles/long travel).
      * [ ] Provide at least one quart per hour per employee.
* Thresholds
   * Indoor
      * [ ] 82°F Trigger: Implement cooling measures (AC/Misting) and cool-down areas <82°F.
   * Outdoor
      * [ ] 80°F Trigger: Provide shade structures close to work area.
      * [ ] 95°F Trigger: Implement High Heat procedures (Pre-shift meetings, Buddy system).
4. Personnel Records: Safety Training Metadata (SB 513)
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1198.5 Mandate: > "Ensure those records include... the name of the employee; the name of the training provider; the duration and date... [and] core competencies."
A. Documentation Standards
* Metadata
   * Capture
      * [ ] Record specific Core Competencies (e.g., "Forklift Model X Operation").
      * [ ] Record name of trainer and exact duration.
* Access
   * Production
      * [ ] Ensure records can be produced within 30 days of written request.
   * Retention
      * [ ] Maintain records for at least 3 years post-termination.
5. Mandatory Facility Postings
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1102.8 Mandate: > "Prominently display in lettering larger than size 14 point type a list of employees' rights... under the whistleblower laws."
A. Standards
* Formatting
   * Whistleblower
      * [ ] Audit poster for 14-point typeface compliance.
* Hygiene
   * Proximity
      * [ ] Provide hand-washing and lactation sinks within "close proximity" (5-7 min walk).
