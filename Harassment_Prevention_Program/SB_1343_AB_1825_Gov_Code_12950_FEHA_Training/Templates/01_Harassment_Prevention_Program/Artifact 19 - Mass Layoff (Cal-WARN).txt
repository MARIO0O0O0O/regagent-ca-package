California Mass Layoff (Cal-WARN) Compliance Checklist (2026)
Version: 5.0 (Toolkit Upgrade) Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Primary Mandate: Cal. Lab. Code § 1400-1408 & SB 617
1. Statutory Triggers and Covered Establishments
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1400 Mandate: > "‘Covered establishment’ means... facility... that employs, or has employed within the preceding 12 months, 75 or more persons... ‘Mass layoff’ means a layoff during any 30-day period of 50 or more employees."
A. Jurisdictional Determination
* Headcount Audit
   * Thresholds
      * [ ] Verify if facility employed 75+ persons in preceding 12 months.
      * [ ] Define "covered establishment" as specific facility or part thereof.
* Trigger Events
   * Mass Layoff
      * [ ] Trigger: 50+ employees separated in 30 days.
   * Relocation/Termination
      * [ ] Trigger: Move 100+ miles away.
      * [ ] Trigger: Cessation of operations (even if <50 employees affected).
2. Notification Period and Recipient Matrix
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1401(a) Mandate: > "60 days before the order takes effect... notice... to the [employees], the Employment Development Department, the local workforce development board, and the chief elected official."
A. Lead Time Compliance
* The 60-Day Rule
   * Delivery
      * [ ] Ensure receipt exactly 60 days before first separation.
      * [ ] Serve notice to employees on leave (PDL/CFRA/Workers' Comp).
* Filings
   * Government
      * [ ] File with EDD.
      * [ ] File with Local Workforce Development Board (LWDB).
      * [ ] Notify Mayor (City) or Board of Supervisors Chair (County).
3. Mandatory Content and Social Services (SB 617)
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1401(b)-(e) Mandate: > "Include... whether the employer plans to coordinate services... through the local workforce development board... [and] a description of... CalFresh."
A. Local Board Integration
* Service Coordination
   * Disclosure
      * [ ] Explicitly state intent to coordinate Rapid Response services.
      * [ ] Arrange services within 30 days of notice if coordinating.
   * Transparency
      * [ ] Include functional email/phone of Local Workforce Dev Board.
      * [ ] Include verbatim description: "Local Workforce Development Boards and their partners help laid off workers find new jobs..."
B. Food Assistance (CalFresh)
* Mandatory Disclosures
   * CalFresh
      * [ ] Provide required description of CalFresh program.
      * [ ] List official benefits helpline and website link.
   * Contact
      * [ ] Provide functioning phone/email for company official.
4. Enforcement and Liability
Legal Authority: Cal. Lab. Code § 1402 & 1403 Mandate: > "Liable to each employee... for back pay [and] benefits... and a civil penalty of not more than $500 for each day."
A. Financial Risk
* Damages
   * Back Pay
      * [ ] Calculate pay at final rate (min $16.90/hr) for every day notice fell short of 60.
      * [ ] Restore value of benefits (health/pension) for violation period.
   * Penalties
      * [ ] Avoid $500/day civil penalty for failure to notify local government.
* Waivers (AB 692)
   * Severance
      * [ ] Ensure severance is "valuable consideration" beyond final wages.
      * [ ] Ensure no "debt repayment" for training/relocation is included in release.
