California Harassment Prevention Compliance Checklist (2026)
Version: 2.0 (Updated with Legal Cross-References) Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Applicability: ALL California Employers (5+ employees for training; 1+ for harassment prohibition)
1. Statutory Training Requirements (SB 1343)
Mandatory for employers with 5 or more employees.
Supervisory Employees
* [ ] Duration: Minimum 2 hours of classroom or interactive e-learning.
* [ ] Frequency: Once every 2 years (biennial).
* [ ] New Hires: Within 6 months of assumption of supervisory position.
* [ ] Content: Sexual harassment, abusive conduct (bullying), and harassment based on gender identity/expression.
Non-Supervisory Employees
* [ ] Duration: Minimum 1 hour.
* [ ] Frequency: Once every 2 years.
* [ ] New Hires: Within 6 months of hire.
* [ ] Seasonal/Temp: Within 30 calendar days or 100 hours worked (whichever is first).
Trainer Qualifications
* [ ] Must be Attorney, HR Professional (2+ yrs exp), Harassment Consultant, or Professor/Instructor.
2. Policy & Prevention Requirements (2 CCR § 11023)
Must be in writing and distributed to all employees.
* [ ] Written Policy: Develop a harassment, discrimination, and retaliation prevention policy.
   * [ ] List all protected categories under FEHA.
   * [ ] Create a complaint process (confidentiality, timely response, impartial investigation).
   * [ ] Prohibit Retaliation: Explicitly state no adverse action for reporting.
* [ ] Distribution Methods (Select at least one):
   * [ ] Physical copy with acknowledgment.
   * [ ] E-mail with read receipt.
   * [ ] Intranet link (with tracking).
* [ ] Bystander Intervention: Training must specifically cover bystander intervention techniques (SB 778).
3. CROSS-REFERENCE: HOW OTHER LAWS IMPACT HARASSMENT PREVENTION
3.1 LEAVE LAWS → HARASSMENT PREVENTION
Legal Principle: Taking protected leave (PDL, CFRA, FMLA) is a protected activity. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Policy: Policy must state "exercise of rights under leave laws" is protected.
* [ ] Deadline Extension: Pause training deadlines if employee is on protected leave.
   * [ ] Action: Schedule training for immediately upon return (within 30 days). Statutory Basis: Cal. Gov. Code § 12945. Cross-Reference: → [Leave Checklist 1-49]
3.2 SB 513 (RECORDKEEPING) → HARASSMENT PREVENTION
Legal Principle: Training records are "Personnel Records" requiring 5 specific metadata fields. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Update Log: Ensure tracking captures:
   1. Employee Name
   2. Provider
   3. Date & Duration
   4. Core Competencies ("Sexual Harassment Prevention, Abusive Conduct, Bystander Intervention")
   5. Certificate details. Statutory Basis: Cal. Lab. Code § 1198.5. Cross-Reference: → [Personnel Records Checklist]
3.3 WORKPLACE VIOLENCE (SB 553) → HARASSMENT PREVENTION
Legal Principle: Threats of violence often start as harassment. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Unified Reporting: Ensure Harassment Complaint Form and Violent Incident Log cross-reference.
* [ ] Threat Assessment: Train supervisors that "threats" are both harassment and safety issues. Statutory Basis: Cal. Lab. Code § 6401.9. Cross-Reference: → [Workplace Violence Checklist]
3.4 PERSONNEL RECORDS → HARASSMENT PREVENTION
Legal Principle: Investigative files must be segregated. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] File Segregation:
   * [ ] Container 1: Certificate of Completion.
   * [ ] Container 5: Complaints, witness notes, investigation reports. Cross-Reference: → [Personnel Records Checklist]
3.5 AB 692 (STAY-OR-PAY) → HARASSMENT PREVENTION
Legal Principle: Employers cannot charge for mandatory training. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] No Repayment: You cannot include harassment training costs in any repayment agreement.
* [ ] Paid Time: Training time is "hours worked" and must be paid. Cross-Reference: → [AB 692 Stay-or-Pay Checklist]
4. Penalties
* DFEH/CRD Order: Mandate to conduct training/policy changes.
* Damages: Unlimited compensatory and punitive damages in civil court.
* Strict Liability: For supervisor harassment.
