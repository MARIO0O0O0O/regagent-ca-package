## HOW TO REPORT

**Safety hazard:** Tell your supervisor or the Program Administrator right away. Use the Hazard Report form (IIPP-FORM-003) if needed.

**Work injury or near-miss:** Report to your supervisor immediately — the same day it happens, no matter how minor. Complete IIPP-FORM-004.

**Cal/OSHA:** You may contact Cal/OSHA at any time. You do not need to go through internal channels first.

## HEAT ILLNESS — KNOW THE SIGNS

**Early Warning:**
- Muscle cramps, headache, dizziness
- Heavy sweating, pale/clammy skin

**Heat Exhaustion:**
- Weakness, nausea, fast/weak pulse
- Cool down immediately — report to supervisor — seek medical eval

**Heat Stroke — CALL 911:**
- Hot skin (may or may not be sweating)
- Confusion, slurred speech, loss of consciousness
- **This is a life-threatening emergency**

**Prevention:** Drink ~1 quart of water per hour in heat. Use shade. Take cool-down breaks. Never ignore heat symptoms.
