# CALIFORNIA WAGE & HOUR QUICK REFERENCE
## HR and Supervisor Reference Tool

**Effective January 1, 2026**  
**Authority: California Labor Code; IWC Wage Orders**

---

## MINIMUM WAGE — 2026

| Jurisdiction | Rate | Notes |
|-------------|------|-------|
| **California (statewide)** | **$16.90/hr** | All employers, all sizes |
| City of Los Angeles | $17.28/hr (est.) | Verify with LA City |
| Unincorporated LA County | $17.81/hr | Applies per hour worked in area |
| Fast food workers (AB 1228) | $20.00/hr | Covered establishments only |

**Exempt salary threshold (2026): $70,304/year ($1,352/week)**  
Recalculate every January 1 when minimum wage increases.

## OVERTIME RATES — CALIFORNIA

| Situation | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Over 8 hours in a workday | **1.5×** regular rate |
| Over 12 hours in a workday | **2×** regular rate |
| 7th consecutive day — first 8 hours | **1.5×** regular rate |
| 7th consecutive day — over 8 hours | **2×** regular rate |
| Over 40 hours in a workweek | **1.5×** regular rate |

**Regular rate = all remuneration for employment (including non-discretionary bonuses, shift differentials)**  
Federal FLSA overtime (weekly only) does not override California's daily overtime rules.

## MEAL PERIOD RULES

| Hours Worked | Meal Periods Required | Timing |
|-------------|----------------------|--------|
| 5 hours or less | 0 | — |
| More than 5 hours | 1 (30 min) | Must begin before end of hour 5 |
| More than 10 hours | 2 (30 min each) | 1st before hour 5; 2nd before hour 10 |

**Valid meal period requires:**
- Employee completely relieved of ALL duties
- Free to leave the premises
- At least 30 uninterrupted minutes

**Waiver (written, mutual agreement):**
- Shift ≤ 6 hours: may waive first meal period
- Shift ≤ 12 hours: may waive second meal period ONLY IF first was not waived

**Premium pay if meal period not provided:** 1 hour at regular rate of pay per occurrence

---

## REST PERIOD RULES

| Hours in Shift | Rest Periods Owed |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Less than 3.5 hours | 0 |
| 3.5 – 7 hours | 1 (10 min paid) |
| 7 – 11 hours | 2 (10 min paid each) |
| 11 – 14 hours | 3 (10 min paid each) |
| 14 – 17 hours | 4 (10 min paid each) |

**Rest periods are PAID. Employee stays on the clock.**  
**Premium pay if rest period not provided:** 1 hour at regular rate per occurrence

## MEAL + REST BREAK TIMELINE (Typical 8-Hour Shift: 8 AM – 5 PM)

```
8:00 AM ──────── 10:00 AM ──────── 12:00 PM ──── 1:00 PM ──────── 3:00 PM ──── 5:00 PM
  Start          Rest #1 (10 min)   Meal (30 min)  Return           Rest #2 (10 min)   End
```
- Rest #1: Approximately 10:00 AM (middle of first 4-hour work period)
- Meal: Must begin by 1:00 PM (before end of 5th hour worked — 5 hours after 8:00 AM)
- Rest #2: Approximately 3:00 PM (middle of second 4-hour work period)

## PAYDAY REQUIREMENTS

| Employee Type | Pay Frequency |
|--------------|--------------|
| Non-exempt (hourly) | At least **twice per month** |
| Exempt (salaried) | Twice per month or monthly (per agreement) |

## FINAL PAY TIMING

| Situation | When Pay Is Due |
|-----------|----------------|
| Employer terminates employee | **Immediately** at time of termination |
| Employee resigns — 72+ hrs notice | Last day of work |
| Employee resigns — less than 72 hrs notice | Within 72 hours |

**Waiting time penalty:** 1 day's wages per day late, up to 30 days (Labor Code § 203)  
**2026 maximum exposure per employee:** $16.90 × 8 hrs × 30 days = **$4,056**

**Always pay out:** Accrued vacation/PTO (treated as wages — cannot be forfeited)  
**Not required to pay out:** Accrued sick leave (unless company policy provides otherwise)

## PAY STUB CHECKLIST (Labor Code § 226)

Every pay stub must contain:
- [ ] Gross wages earned this pay period
- [ ] Total hours worked (non-exempt employees)
- [ ] Each deduction itemized separately
- [ ] Net wages
- [ ] Start and end dates of pay period
- [ ] Employee name + last 4 of SSN or employee ID
- [ ] Employer name and address
- [ ] Hourly rate(s) in effect + corresponding hours at each rate (non-exempt)
- [ ] **Paid sick leave balance** (or provided on separate document)

**Missing any item:** $50 per employee initial violation; $100 subsequent; up to $4,000 per employee + PAGA

---

## WAGE THEFT PREVENTION NOTICE (Labor Code § 2810.5)

Required for all non-exempt employees at hire and within 7 days of any change.

Notice must include:
- [ ] Rate of pay and basis (hourly, salary, piece-rate, etc.)
- [ ] Allowances (if any)
- [ ] Regular payday
- [ ] Employer legal name, DBA, address, main phone
- [ ] Workers' comp insurer name, address, phone
- [ ] Paid sick leave availability
- [ ] Applicable minimum wage
- [ ] Any disaster declaration in the county within 30 days of hire

**Free template:** www.dir.ca.gov/dlse/lc_2810.5.html  
**Update required by January 2026** to reflect $16.90 minimum wage

---

## EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT (Labor Code § 2802)

Must reimburse:
- Mileage (IRS rate or actual cost, whichever is higher)
- Required cell phone use (reasonable percentage of monthly plan)
- Required home internet for remote employees
- All necessary work-related expenditures

**Cannot be waived by agreement. Cannot be offset against wages.**

## PAGA PENALTIES QUICK REFERENCE

| Violation | Initial Penalty | Subsequent |
|-----------|----------------|------------|
| Pay stub deficiency | $100/employee/pay period | $200/employee/pay period |
| Minimum wage violation | $100/employee/pay period | $250/employee/pay period |
| Missed meal period (no premium) | $100/employee/pay period | $200/employee/pay period |
| Missed rest period (no premium) | $100/employee/pay period | $200/employee/pay period |

**PAGA notice received?** Consult legal counsel immediately. 65-day response window. 33-day cure opportunity.
