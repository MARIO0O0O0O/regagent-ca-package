**PART II: Video Scripts & Concepts (3 Videos)**

**Video 1: What is Workplace Violence?** (3 minutes)
- Scene 1: Office setting - narrator defines workplace violence per SB 553
- Definition: "Any act or threat of physical violence, harassment, intimidation, or other threatening disruptive behavior that occurs at the work site"
- Scene 2: Show 4 types with quick vignettes (robbery, angry customer, coworker conflict, domestic spillover)
- Scene 3: Statistics and why prevention matters
- Closing: "This training will teach you to recognize warning signs and respond safely"

**Video 2: De-Escalation Techniques** (5 minutes)
- Scene 1: Customer service counter - customer becoming agitated
- Show WRONG approach: defensive posture, arguing, dismissing concerns
- Scene 2: Rewind and show RIGHT approach:
  - **S**tay calm - control your breathing, neutral tone
  - **A**cknowledge concerns - "I understand you're frustrated"
  - **F**ind common ground - "Let's figure this out together"
  - **E**xit strategy - create physical space, offer alternatives
  - **R**eport if escalates - know when to call for help
- Scene 3: Practice scenarios with multiple outcomes
- Acronym: **SAFER** de-escalation

**Video 3: Active Shooter Response - RUN HIDE FIGHT** (4 minutes)
- Scene 1: Office building - active shooter situation begins
- **RUN**: If you can safely escape
  - Leave belongings behind
  - Help others if possible but don't wait
  - Call 911 once safe
- **HIDE**: If escape route blocked
  - Lock/barricade doors
  - Turn off lights, silence phones
  - Spread out, stay quiet
- **FIGHT**: Only as last resort
  - Use any objects as weapons
  - Commit to incapacitating attacker
  - Work together
- Scene 4: Law enforcement arrival protocols
- Closing: "Hope you never need this, but be prepared"

**PART III: Interactive Discussion Scenarios (8 Scenarios)**

**Scenario 1: The Angry Customer** (5 minutes)
- **Description**: Retail store, customer denied refund without receipt. Customer raises voice: "This is ridiculous! I want to speak to your manager NOW! You people are all incompetent!" Customer slams fist on counter. Other customers watching nervously.
- **Discussion Prompts**:
  1. What type of workplace violence is this? (Type 2: Customer/Client)
  2. What are the warning signs of potential escalation?
  3. How should employee respond using SAFER de-escalation?
  4. At what point should employee call for backup/security?
  5. Is this reportable under SB 553?
- **Facilitator Analysis**:
  - Type 2 violence (customer-on-worker)
  - Warning signs: raised voice, physical aggression (fist slam), threatening language
  - SAFER approach: Stay calm, acknowledge frustration, find solution, create exit strategy
  - Call manager/security if customer makes physical threats or attempts violence
  - YES reportable: threat of physical violence occurred (fist slam is intimidation)

**Scenario 2: Coworker Making Threats** (5 minutes)
- **Description**: Break room, employee Mark has been written up for tardiness. Mark tells coworker: "I'm so sick of this place. One more write-up and I'm going to lose it. They have no idea what I'm capable of." Mark has been increasingly isolated, stopped socializing with team.
- **Discussion Prompts**:
  1. What type of workplace violence risk is this?
  2. What behavioral warning signs are present?
  3. What should the coworker who heard this do?
  4. Is this a reportable threat?
  5. What should the employer do?
- **Facilitator Analysis**:
  - Type 3 risk (worker-on-worker)
  - Warning signs: vague threats ("what I'm capable of"), social withdrawal, expressed grievances
  - Coworker MUST report to supervisor/HR immediately (bystander responsibility)
  - YES reportable: threatening language even if non-specific
  - Employer must: document in violent incident log, conduct threat assessment, consider Employee Assistance Program (EAP) referral, monitor situation
