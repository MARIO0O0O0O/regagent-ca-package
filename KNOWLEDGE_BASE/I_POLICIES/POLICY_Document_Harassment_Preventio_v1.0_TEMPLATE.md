## 1. PURPOSE AND POLICY STATEMENT

[COMPANY NAME] is committed to providing a workplace free from harassment, discrimination, and retaliation. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, customers, and visitors.

**Zero Tolerance:** We maintain a zero-tolerance policy for harassment of any kind. All employees have the right to work in an environment free from harassment based on protected characteristics.

**Scope:** This policy covers conduct that occurs:
- In the workplace
- At work-related events
- During business travel
- In work-related communications (email, text, social media)
- Between employees outside of work if it affects the work environment

## 2. PROHIBITED CONDUCT

### A. Sexual Harassment Defined

**Quid Pro Quo Sexual Harassment:**
Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when:
- Submission is made a term or condition of employment
- Submission or rejection is used as basis for employment decisions
- Such conduct affects an individual's employment

**Hostile Work Environment Sexual Harassment:**
Unwelcome sexual conduct that is severe or pervasive enough to create a work environment that a reasonable person would consider intimidating, hostile, or abusive.

### B. Examples of Sexual Harassment

**Verbal:**
- Sexual comments about body, appearance, or clothing
- Sexual jokes, innuendos, or propositions
- Repeated requests for dates after being told no
- Sexual rumors or gossip
- Whistling, catcalling, or sexual sounds
- Questions about sexual activity or preferences
- Comments about sexual orientation or gender identity

**Physical:**
- Unwanted touching, hugging, kissing, or patting
- Blocking movement or cornering someone
- Sexual gestures
- Assault or attempted assault

**Visual:**
- Displaying or sharing sexual images (posters, calendars, emails, texts)
- Staring at body parts ("elevator eyes")
- Sexual gestures

**Digital:**
- Sexual texts, emails, or instant messages
- Sharing sexual images or videos
- Sexual content on social media
- Using company technology to view pornography

### C. Non-Sexual Harassment

Harassment based on other protected characteristics is also prohibited:
- Race, color, national origin, ancestry
- Religion or religious creed
- Age (40 and over)
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Medical condition (cancer, genetic characteristics)
- Marital status
- Sex, pregnancy, childbirth, breastfeeding
- Sexual orientation
- Gender, gender identity, gender expression
- Military or veteran status
- Genetic information

**Examples:**
- Racial slurs, epithets, or derogatory comments
- Ethnic jokes or stereotypes
- Religious mockery or proselytizing
- Age-related comments ("too old," "past your prime")
- Disability-related jokes or mimicking
- Pregnancy discrimination
- Anti-LGBTQ+ comments or "jokes"

### D. Abusive Conduct (Workplace Bullying)

California law requires training on "abusive conduct" including:
- Repeated mistreatment
- Verbal abuse (yelling, insults, put-downs)
- Threatening, intimidating, or humiliating behavior
- Work sabotage
- Creating a hostile work environment

**Important:** Abusive conduct is prohibited even when not based on protected characteristics.

## 3. PROTECTED CLASSES UNDER CALIFORNIA LAW

California provides broader protections than federal law. The following are protected classes under FEHA:

1. **Race** - Includes hair texture and protective hairstyles (CROWN Act)
2. **Religious Creed** - Includes religious dress and grooming
3. **Color** - Skin color or complexion
4. **National Origin** - Includes accent, language, ethnicity
5. **Ancestry** - Family origin or heritage
6. **Physical Disability** - Includes perceived disability
7. **Mental Disability** - Includes learning disabilities
8. **Medical Condition** - Cancer, genetic characteristics
9. **Genetic Information** - Family medical history
10. **Marital Status** - Single, married, divorced, widowed, domestic partner
11. **Sex** - Includes pregnancy, childbirth, breastfeeding
12. **Gender** - Gender identity and expression
13. **Age** - 40 years and older
14. **Sexual Orientation** - Heterosexual, homosexual, bisexual
15. **Military and Veteran Status** - Service members and veterans

## 6. INVESTIGATION PROCESS

### A. Prompt Investigation

All reports will be investigated **promptly and thoroughly**:
- Investigation begins within [3] business days
- Completed within [30] days (or as soon as reasonably possible)
- Complainant and respondent kept informed of progress

### B. Investigation Steps

1. **Initial Review**
   - HR assesses complaint
   - Determines if investigation needed
   - Identifies interim protective measures

2. **Interviews**
   - Complainant (detailed statement)
   - Respondent (opportunity to respond)
   - Witnesses (anyone with relevant information)
   - Additional interviews as needed

3. **Evidence Collection**
   - Emails, texts, social media
   - Performance reviews, disciplinary records
   - Surveillance footage (if applicable)
   - Any physical evidence

4. **Analysis**
   - Credibility assessment
   - Consistency of statements
   - Corroborating evidence
   - Pattern of behavior

5. **Conclusion**
   - Substantiated (harassment occurred)
   - Unsubstantiated (insufficient evidence)
   - Inconclusive (conflicting evidence)

### C. Confidentiality

- Information shared only on **need-to-know basis**
- Necessary for investigation, corrective action, legal compliance
- Not absolute - some disclosure required for fair investigation
- Files maintained separately from personnel records

### D. Interim Measures

During investigation, we may implement:
- Schedule changes
- Workspace relocation
- Paid administrative leave
- No-contact directives
- Escort services

## 7. CORRECTIVE ACTION AND DISCIPLINE

### A. If Harassment is Substantiated

**Disciplinary action will be taken based on severity:**

**Minor First Offense:**
- Written warning
- Mandatory retraining
- Performance improvement plan
- Loss of privileges

**Moderate/Repeated Offense:**
- Suspension without pay
- Demotion
- Transfer
- Final written warning

**Severe Offense:**
- Immediate termination
- Law enforcement referral (if criminal)

**Factors Considered:**
- Severity of conduct
- Frequency
- Prior disciplinary history
- Position/authority of harasser
- Impact on victim and workplace
- Evidence of remorse or corrective action

### B. Other Outcomes

**If Unsubstantiated:**
- No discipline to respondent
- Training may still be recommended
- Monitoring for retaliation

**If False Accusation:**
- If evidence shows deliberately false report
- Complainant may face discipline
- Honest but mistaken reports are NOT false accusations

## 8. NON-RETALIATION POLICY

### A. Protected Activity

It is **illegal** to retaliate against anyone who:
- Reports harassment (in good faith)
- Participates in an investigation
- Opposes harassment
- Assists someone else in reporting

### B. What is Retaliation?

**Prohibited retaliatory actions include:**
- Termination, demotion, or discipline
- Pay reduction or denial of benefits
- Schedule changes or shift reassignments
- Undesirable work assignments
- Exclusion from meetings or communications
- Negative performance reviews
- Hostile treatment, shunning, or gossip
- Threats or intimidation

### C. Report Retaliation Immediately

- Use same reporting procedures as harassment
- Retaliation is a **separate violation**
- Subject to discipline up to termination
- May result in personal liability

## 9. SUPERVISOR RESPONSIBILITIES

**All supervisors and managers must:**

**Prevent:**
- Model appropriate behavior
- Address inappropriate conduct immediately
- Create respectful work environment
- Be alert to harassment signs

**Report:**
- Report any harassment you witness or learn about
- Cannot ignore or handle informally
- Mandatory reporting within [24 hours]
- Failure to report = discipline

**Respond:**
- Take complaints seriously
- Do not investigate yourself (let HR)
- Do not promise confidentiality (can't guarantee)
- Do not retaliate against complainants

**Cooperate:**
- Participate in investigations
- Implement corrective actions
- Document incidents
- Attend required training

### [POSITION] Liability

California law allows **personal liability** for supervisors who:
- Commit harassment
- Know about harassment and don't report it
- Retaliate against complainants
- Discourage reporting

You can be sued **personally** even if company is also sued.

## 10. BYSTANDER INTERVENTION

**If you witness harassment:**

**The 4 D's:**

1. **Direct Intervention**
   - "That's not okay."
   - "Stop that behavior."
   - "That comment is inappropriate."

2. **Distract**
   - Change the subject
   - Create interruption
   - Ask for help with task

3. **Delegate**
   - Get supervisor or HR
   - Ask someone with authority to intervene
   - Report formally

4. **Delay**
   - Check on victim afterward
   - "Are you okay?"
   - "That was inappropriate, do you want to report it?"
   - Offer to be witness

**You are protected from retaliation for intervening.**

- **Deadline:** 300 days from last incident

### A. Romantic/Sexual Relationships at Work

**Relationships between employees are not prohibited, but:**
- Must be truly consensual
- Cannot involve supervisor and subordinate (power imbalance)
- Must not create hostile environment for others
- Must not interfere with work performance
- Display of affection should be professional

### B. [POSITION]-Subordinate Relationships

**Strictly Prohibited:**
- [POSITION] cannot date direct report
- Must disclose to HR immediately
