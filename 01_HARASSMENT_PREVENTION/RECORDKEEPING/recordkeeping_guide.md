## 1. WHAT RECORDS MUST BE KEPT

### A. Training Records (SB 1343 Requirement)
The employer must maintain records of all harassment prevention training sufficient to demonstrate compliance, including:

| Record | Content | Minimum Retention |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| Training sign-in roster (HP-FORM-002) | Names, dates, duration, session type | 2 years |
| Training completion log (HP-FORM-003) | Master record all employees | 4 years recommended |
| Trainer credentials | Name, title, basis for qualification | Duration of employment + 2 years |
| Training content documentation | Agenda, materials, scenarios used | 2 years |
| Employee training certificates (HP-FORM-005) | Copy in employee file | Duration of employment |

> **Practice:** [COMPANY NAME] retains all training records for **4 years** (double the legal minimum) to cover the CRD's 3-year complaint window.

### B. Policy Acknowledgment Records
| Record | Content | Minimum Retention |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| Policy acknowledgment (HP-FORM-001) | Signed acknowledgment per employee | Duration of employment + 3 years |
| Policy version history | All versions with effective dates | Indefinite |
| Policy distribution records | How and when policy was distributed | Duration of employment + 3 years |

### C. Complaint and Investigation Records
| Record | Content | Minimum Retention |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| Complaint form (HP-FORM-004) | Original complaint document | 7 years |
| Investigation notes | Interviews, evidence, timeline | 7 years |
| Investigation findings report | Written conclusion and basis | 7 years |
| Corrective action records | Discipline, remedies, monitoring | 7 years |
| Communications (emails, texts) | All investigation-related comms | 7 years |

> **Important:** Complaint files are stored SEPARATELY from personnel files. They are not placed in the accused's general personnel file and are accessible only to HR and legal counsel on a need-to-know basis.

## 2. WHERE RECORDS ARE STORED

### Filing Structure
```
HR COMPLIANCE FILES
├── TRAINING/
│   ├── Sign-In Rosters (by date)
│   ├── Training Logs (HP-FORM-003 master)
│   └── Trainer Credentials
│
├── ACKNOWLEDGMENTS/
│   └── [Employee Name] - Acknowledgment + Date
│
└── COMPLAINTS/ (RESTRICTED ACCESS)
    └── [Case Number] - [Year]
        ├── Complaint Form
        ├── Investigation File
        ├── Findings Report
        └── Corrective Action Record
```

### Access Controls
- **Training records:** HR and authorized managers
- **Policy acknowledgments:** HR; employee may access their own
- **Complaint files:** HR director and designated investigators only; legal counsel as needed

---

## 3. PRIVACY AND CONFIDENTIALITY

- Complaint files are never placed in general personnel files
- Complaint files are password-protected if stored electronically
- Access log maintained for complaint files
- Information shared only on need-to-know basis during investigation
- Even after investigation closes, complaint files remain confidential

## 4. ANNUAL COMPLIANCE AUDIT CHECKLIST

Complete annually or when preparing for a regulatory inspection.

**Training Compliance:**
- [ ] All employees trained within required timeframe (within 6 months of hire; every 2 years)
- [ ] Supervisors trained with 2-hour session (not 1-hour)
- [ ] Training records complete for all employees
- [ ] New hires tracked for 6-month training deadline
- [ ] Trainer qualifications documented

**Policy Compliance:**
- [ ] Current policy version posted / distributed
- [ ] Policy acknowledgments collected from all current employees
- [ ] Acknowledgments collected after most recent policy update
- [ ] Policy reflects current law (CRD not DFEH; current statutes)

**Complaint Compliance:**
- [ ] All complaints documented in complaint log
- [ ] Investigation initiated within 3 business days for all complaints
- [ ] Investigations completed within 30 days
- [ ] Investigation files complete and properly restricted
- [ ] No complaint files in general personnel files
- [ ] Corrective action implemented and documented for substantiated complaints

**Records:**
- [ ] Retention schedule followed; no premature destruction
- [ ] Destroyed records documented per destruction log

## 6. CALIFORNIA CRD INVESTIGATION REQUIREMENTS

If a CRD or EEOC investigation is initiated:

- **Preserve all records** related to the complaint immediately — do not destroy any document that may be relevant
- **Legal hold** notice issued to all custodians of potentially relevant records
- **Do not alter** any document once investigation is underway
- Consult legal counsel before responding to investigation requests
- Retain all records for the duration of the investigation + 7 years
