California Workplace Violence Prevention Plan (WVPP) Checklist (SB 553)
Version: 2.0 (Updated with Legal Cross-References) Effective Date: July 1, 2024 (Enforceable Now) Applicability: ALL California Employers
1. Written Plan Requirements (Cal. Lab. Code § 6401.9)
* [ ] Plan Format: Standalone document OR section in IIPP.
* [ ] Responsible Persons: Designate specific individuals.
* [ ] Employee Involvement: Procedures for active employee participation in plan development.
* [ ] Emergency Response: Protocols for alerting employees and law enforcement.
* [ ] Communication: How to report incidents without fear of retaliation.
2. Violent Incident Log (Mandatory)
Must be separate from OSHA 300 log.
* [ ] Content: Date, time, location, description, perpetrator type (1-4), circumstances, consequences.
* [ ] Privacy: NO personal identifying information of any person involved.
* [ ] Retention: 5 years.
3. Training Requirements
* [ ] Initial & Annual: Training provided when plan established and annually thereafter.
* [ ] Content: How to report, hazard identification, specific hazards of the job, the Log.
* [ ] Interactive: Must offer Q&A opportunity.
4. Hazard Identification
* [ ] Inspections: Periodic inspections to identify unsafe conditions (lighting, escape routes).
* [ ] Correction: Tracking of when hazards were fixed.
5. CROSS-REFERENCE: HOW OTHER LAWS IMPACT WORKPLACE VIOLENCE
5.1 HARASSMENT PREVENTION → WORKPLACE VIOLENCE
Legal Principle: Overlapping reporting channels. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Combined Training: Allowed if distinct requirements met.
* [ ] Escalation: "Abusive conduct" complaints may escalate to violence threats. Cross-Reference: → [Harassment Prevention Checklist]
5.2 WORKERS' COMPENSATION → WORKPLACE VIOLENCE
Legal Principle: Violence injuries are work injuries. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Claim Filing: Provide DWC-1 form within 1 day of injury from violence. Statutory Basis: Cal. Lab. Code § 3200. Cross-Reference: → [Workers' Comp Checklist]
5.3 CAL/OSHA IIPP → WORKPLACE VIOLENCE
Legal Principle: WVPP is a safety program. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Integration: IIPP must reference the WVPP.
* [ ] Safety Meetings: Include violence topics in safety committee agendas. Cross-Reference: → [Cal/OSHA Safety Checklist]
5.4 SB 513 (TRAINING RECORDS) → WORKPLACE VIOLENCE
Legal Principle: Training records require metadata. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Log Fields: Name, Provider, Date, Core Competencies ("De-escalation, Reporting"), Certification. Cross-Reference: → [Personnel Records Checklist]
5.5 PERSONNEL RECORDS → WORKPLACE VIOLENCE
Legal Principle: Privacy of safety logs. Compliance Impact:
* [ ] Log Separation: Violent Incident Log must be kept SEPARATE from personnel files and anonymized. Cross-Reference: → [Personnel Records Checklist]
6. Penalties
* Cal/OSHA Citations: Serious violations up to $25,000.
* Willful Violations: Up to $158,760.
