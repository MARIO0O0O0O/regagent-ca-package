## 2. DEFINITIONS

### A. Workplace Violence

**Cal/OSHA Definition:**
"Any act of violence or threat of violence that occurs at or is related to a place of employment."

**Includes:**
- Physical assault or battery
- Threats of physical harm
- Aggressive behavior causing reasonable fear
- Intimidation attempts
- Verbal abuse creating fear of harm
- Property damage with violent intent

**Does NOT require:**
- Physical contact (threats alone qualify)
- Injury to occur
- Weapon involvement
- Criminal charges

### B. Types of Workplace Violence

**Type I: Criminal Intent**
- Perpetrator has **no legitimate relationship** to business
- Intent: Commit crime (robbery, burglary, trespassing, vandalism)
- Examples: Store robbery, smash-and-grab, parking lot theft
- Risk Factors: Cash-handling businesses, retail stores, late-night operations, isolated locations

**Type II: Customer/Client**
- Perpetrator is or was **customer, client, patient, student, inmate, or visitor**
- Trigger: Dissatisfaction with service, frustration with wait, mental illness, substance abuse
- Examples: Angry customer, aggressive patient, road rage, hostile client
- Risk Factors: Customer service roles, healthcare, education, hospitality, sales

**Type III: Worker-on-Worker**
- Perpetrator is **current or former employee**
- Trigger: Workplace conflicts, perceived injustices, mental health issues, termination
- Examples: Bullying, fights between coworkers, threatened violence, domestic violence in workplace
- Risk Factors: Layoffs, discipline, interpersonal conflicts, poor management

**Type IV: Personal Relationship**
- Perpetrator has **personal relationship with employee** (domestic partner, family, acquaintance)
- Trigger: Domestic violence, stalking, relationship conflict
- Examples: Abusive spouse comes to workplace, stalker, personal dispute
- Risk Factors: Domestic violence victims, restraining orders, stalking complaints

### C. Threat

**Threat Assessment Levels:**

**Level 1: Vague/Indirect**
- Generalized statements
- "Someone should teach them a lesson"
- "I'm so angry I could..."
- No specific target or plan

**Level 2: Direct**
- Specific target identified
- "I'm going to hurt [name]"
- No detailed plan
- May be conditional ("If they do X, I'll do Y")

**Level 3: Imminent**
- Specific target
- Detailed plan
- Means to carry out (weapon, opportunity)
- Timeline indicated
- Immediate danger

### D. Aggressive Behavior

Conduct that creates reasonable fear including:
- Yelling, screaming, cursing
- Throwing objects
- Slamming doors, banging fists
- Invading personal space
- Blocking exits or pathways
- Making threatening gestures
- Stalking or following
- Unwanted physical contact

## 7. INCIDENT INVESTIGATION

### A. Investigation Required

All workplace violence incidents or threats must be investigated, including:
- Physical assault or battery
- Threats (verbal, written, electronic)
- Aggressive behavior causing fear
- Near-miss situations
- Intimidation or stalking
- Property damage (with violent intent)
- Witnessed violence off-site affecting work

### B. Investigation Process

**Step 1: Immediate Response (Within Hours)**
- Ensure victim safety
- Separate involved parties
- Secure the scene
- Preserve evidence
- Call 911 if emergency

**Step 2: Notification (Within 24 Hours)**
- Notify Plan Administrator
- Notify supervisor/management
- Notify HR
- Document in incident log

**Step 3: Investigation (Within 7 Days)**
- Interview victim
- Interview alleged perpetrator
- Interview witnesses
- Review evidence (videos, emails, texts)
- Assess credibility
- Determine facts

**Step 4: Analysis (Within 14 Days)**
- Classify incident type (I, II, III, IV)
- Identify contributing factors
- Determine if hazard could have been prevented
- Assess need for corrective actions

**Step 5: Report (Within 30 Days)**
- Written investigation report
- Findings and conclusions
- Recommended corrective actions
- Implementation timeline

### C. Investigation Documentation

**Each investigation must document:**
- Date, time, location of incident
- Names of involved parties
- Detailed description of incident
- Classification (Type I, II, III, or IV)
- Witness statements
- Evidence collected
- Contributing factors
- Root cause analysis
- Corrective actions taken
- Follow-up actions needed

### D. Confidentiality

- Information shared on need-to-know basis
- Victim identity protected to extent possible
- Investigation records maintained separately
- Access restricted to authorized personnel

## 8. HAZARD CORRECTION

### A. Correction Timeline

**Imminent Hazards:**
- Corrected **immediately**
- Examples: Active threat, broken lock, failed alarm
- Temporary measures until permanent fix

**Serious Hazards:**
- Corrected within **7 days**
- Examples: Poor lighting, missing panic button
- Interim measures implemented

**Other Hazards:**
- Corrected within **30 days**
- Examples: Training needs, policy updates
- Correction plan documented

### B. Temporary Measures

While permanent corrections are being implemented:
- Increase staffing
- Change schedules
- Escort policies
- Additional security
- Modified procedures
- Restricted access

### C. Corrective Actions by Hazard Type

**Physical Security Improvements:**
- Install/upgrade lighting
- Add locks, access control
- Install surveillance cameras
- Add panic buttons/alarms
- Improve visibility (mirrors, windows)
- Secure cash handling
- Modify workspace layout

**Policy and Procedure Changes:**
- Update safety policies
- Revise customer service protocols
- Implement buddy systems
- Create late-night procedures
- Establish visitor check-in
- Define escalation procedures

**Training and Education:**
- De-escalation training
- Threat recognition
- Emergency response drills
- Self-defense (if appropriate)
- Supervisor training on warning signs

**Staffing Adjustments:**
- Increase staffing during high-risk times
- Eliminate lone worker situations
- Add security personnel
- Change shift assignments

**Technology Solutions:**
- Communication devices (radios, phones)
- Personal safety apps
- Check-in systems for lone workers
- Video monitoring systems

### D. Verification of Corrections

Plan Administrator verifies:
- Correction completed as described
- Correction addresses hazard effectively
- No new hazards created
- Employees informed of changes
- Documentation updated

### E. Tracking

Hazard correction tracking includes:
- Hazard identified date
- Description and location
- Severity classification

- Target completion date
- Actual completion date
- Verification method
- Follow-up actions

## 11. EMERGENCY RESPONSE PROCEDURES

### A. Active Threat Response: RUN-HIDE-FIGHT

**When violence occurs or is imminent:**

**1. RUN (Evacuate if Possible)**
- Have escape route in mind
- Leave belongings behind
- Help others if safe to do so
- Do not attempt to move injured people
- Call 911 when safe
- Prevent others from entering area

**2. HIDE (If Evacuation Not Possible)**
- Find hiding place
- Lock doors, barricade entry
- Turn off lights
- Silence phones
- Spread out (don't cluster)
- Remain quiet
- Do NOT come out until police give "all clear"

**3. FIGHT (As Last Resort)**
- Only if life is in imminent danger
- Act with aggression
- Use any objects as weapons
- Yell loudly
- Commit to your actions
- Work with others if possible

### B. Threat Level Responses

**Level 1: Vague/Indirect Threat**
- Report to supervisor
- Document the threat
- Monitor situation
- No immediate evacuation

**Level 2: Direct Threat**
- Report to Plan Administrator immediately
- May remove threat maker from workplace
- May contact law enforcement
- Assess need for protective measures

**Level 3: Imminent Threat**
- Call 911 immediately
- Evacuate if safe
- Implement Run-Hide-Fight if needed
- Follow law enforcement instructions

### C. After an Incident

**Immediate (Within Hours):**
- Account for all employees
- Provide first aid (if trained)
- Do NOT re-enter until police clear scene
- Gather at designated meeting point: [LOCATION]
- Do NOT disturb evidence

**Short-Term (Within Days):**
- Debrief with employees
- Offer counseling (EAP)
- Complete incident report
- Begin investigation
- Implement interim safety measures

**Long-Term (Within Weeks):**
- Complete investigation
- Implement corrective actions
- Update WVPP if needed
- Provide additional training
- Follow up with affected employees

### D. Law Enforcement Interaction

**When police arrive:**
- Remain calm, follow instructions
- Keep hands visible
- Do NOT make sudden movements
- Do NOT run toward officers
- Provide information when asked
- Expect to be searched/questioned
